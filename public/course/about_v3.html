The Origin Story

The human entry point.

Cognis11 exists because the most capable children are often the most poorly served by standard 11+ preparation — and the reason is identifiable and fixable.

The Problem

Standard 11+ prep is built on a single assumption: more practice produces better results. For many children, that's true. For a specific type of child — one who constructs understanding before acting — high-volume drilling leads to frustration and speed plateaus. They are reasoners being asked to behave like calculators.

These children understand the material. They can explain their reasoning in detail. They consistently get questions right in relaxed conditions. But in timed papers, something goes wrong. They run out of time. Scores plateau. Tutors prescribe more practice. The gap doesn't close.

"The preparation isn't failing because the child isn't working hard enough. It's failing because it was designed for a different type of mind."

The Method

Schema-Based Instruction, repointed.

We adapt Schema-Based Instruction (SBI) — a well-researched pillar of cognitive psychology — to give these children the structural "X-Ray" vision they need to classify problem types in seconds, rather than re-deriving the logic from scratch every time. The research base is extensive. The application to high-achieving children preparing for selective entry is new.

The result: a child who still reasons deeply, but no longer needs to rebuild the scaffold from the ground up under time pressure. Speed follows naturally once the classification is automatic.

Why Cognis11 is parent-led.

A child does not build a new mental model from passive exposure to a screen. They build it through interaction with a trusted adult who can read the moment of confusion and respond to it in real time. This is not a design preference — it is why the learning transfers.

Every module prepares the parent first. In 60 seconds, you understand the concept, the most common error, and the fix. Then you guide your child through the interactive logic engine together. Ten minutes. In the same room. No maths background required. No tutor needed.
