Foundation Progressing

The Space Eraser

In your head, you must learn to erase the gaps. When the spaces vanish, your brain stops reading for meaning and starts seeing the "Seams"—the hot zones where hidden words live.

"Look at 'MADE A FOUL'. It tells a story. But if we click 'Erase Spaces', the story disappears and it becomes a stream of letters. Look at the seam between MADE and A. The word DEAF was hiding there all along!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Seam Secret." We usually see spaces as walls. In the 11+, secret words are "glued" across those gaps. These are Bridge Words—they steal letters from the end of one word and the start of the next. To find them, treat the whole sentence as a continuous character stream.

Level 1 "Find the 3-letter word hidden in: 'THE ATOM'."
Level 2 "Find the 4-letter word hidden in: 'FISH ARE DEEP'."
Level 3 "Find the 4-letter word hidden in: 'MADE A FOUL'."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Seam Scanner. Move the beam across the character stream. Notice that the "ghost word" doesn't care about the original meaning. It only cares about the sequence of letters at the junction.

Phase 3: The Engine

Type a phrase and use the Scanner Position to hunt for bridges. Adjust the window width for longer words. Watch for the moment a real word illuminates in the gold beam.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Inside Job" Trap: Most children look for a word *inside* a single big word (like CAT in SCATTER). In the 11+, the hidden word is almost never inside a single word.

The Fix: The "Pinch" Method. "The answer lives in the cracks." Put a finger over the space. Look at the 2 letters before and the 2 letters after. That junction is the 90% Hot Zone. **Ignore the centers, scan the seams.**
