Module 13: Train & Tunnel

Foundation Intro

The Caterpillar's Tail

In the 11+, a train isn't a tiny dot. It’s long! Imagine a caterpillar crawling under a leaf. To be "completely through," its nose has to come out, but its **tail** has to follow. The journey isn't finished until the very last bit clears the exit.

"Look at our caterpillar. He's 4 segments long. Click 'Crawl' and watch his nose reach the end of the leaf. Is he through yet? No! He has to keep crawling until his tail is out. That 'extra crawl' is the length of his own body."

Foundation Progressing

The Measuring Tape

To find the True Distance, we use a simple rule: **Add the Train to the Tunnel**. We don't just measure the tunnel; we measure from where the nose starts to where the tail finishes. This "Stretched Distance" is the secret to getting the math right.

"Adjust the sliders. Watch the red measuring bracket at the top. It doesn't just cover the tunnel; it covers the tunnel **plus** the extra space the train needs to pull its tail out. That’s our 'True Distance'."

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+, a train isn't a point—it’s a long object. For a train to pass a tunnel or platform, the nose has to enter, but the tail has to clear the exit. This means the total distance is the length of the obstacle plus the length of the train itself.

The Signal Post "A 50m train passes a signal post. How far does the front travel before the back clears?"
The Mountain Tunnel "A 100m train goes through a 300m tunnel. Find the total distance covered."
Speed Challenge "A 200m train passes a 400m platform in 30s. What is the speed in m/s?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Notice the Total Journey. The distance isn't just the dark tunnel box. It’s the entire path from the moment the "nose" touches the entrance to the moment the "tail" disappears. The train travels its own length just to get its bottom out of the door.

Phase 3: The Engine

Adjust the lengths. Watch how the True Distance is always the sum of both. The math is only finished when the status says "TAIL CLEARED."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Front-Door" Trap: Children often use only the tunnel length (e.g. 400m). At 400m, the nose is out, but the body is still stuck inside!

The Fix: The "String and Tube" Rule. Imagine pulling a 2m string through a 4m tube. To get it all out, you pull 6m. **Always add the Train + Tunnel.**
