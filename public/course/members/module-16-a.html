Module 16: The "More Than" Trap

Foundation Intro

The Secret Bonus

If you have 10 stickers to share, but you want to give Sarah **2 more** than Tom, you can't just split them 5 and 5. If you do that, Sarah doesn't have more! The secret is to give Sarah her "Bonus" first, then share the rest fairly.

"We have 10 stickers. Sarah needs 2 more than Tom. First, click 'Give Bonus'—we put Sarah's 2 stickers to one side. Now, look at the 8 stickers left. We share those 4 for Sarah and 4 for Tom. Sarah ends up with 6 and Tom has 4. Perfect!"

Foundation Progressing

The Tower Haircut

When you have two uneven towers and you know their Combined Height, the math is tricky. But if you give the tall tower a "Haircut" by snipping off the extra bit, the towers become identical twins. Once they are twins, you can divide by 2!

"These towers are 20 blocks tall combined. Tower A is 4 blocks taller. If we 'Snip' those 4 blocks off, the 16 blocks left must be split exactly 8 and 8. Now we know Tower B is 8, and Tower A is 8 plus the 4 we snipped!"
Combined: 20 blocks. Gap: 4 blocks.

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "Fake 50/50" problem. It occurs when you have a Combined Total and a Gap (the difference). Your instinct is to divide the total by 2—don't! If you divide first, the math won't balance. The secret is to remove the "thief's portion" (the gap) before you touch the divide key.

The Stickers "Two friends have 12 stickers. Sarah has 4 more than Tom. How many for Tom?"
The Tablet "Tablet and case cost £250. Tablet is £200 more than the case. Find the case."
The Ages "Father and son are 50 combined. Father is 26 years older. How old is the son?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

To beat the trap, we "Snip the Gap." If we physically cut the extra pile off and put it aside, the two stacks left are exactly equal. Everything to the left of the red dashed line is a Fair Share. This is the only way to find the smaller value without guessing.

Phase 3: The Engine

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Impulse Split": If a £250 total has a £200 gap, children say: "Case £50, Tablet £200." Trap! £200 is only £150 more than £50. The gap failed!

The Fix: The "Snip and Share" Rule. Subtract the gap first: £250 - £200 = £50. Share the rest: £50 ÷ 2 = £25. The case is £25. The tablet is £25 + £200 = £225. **Always Snip before you Split.**
