Module 40: Odd One Out

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as the "Group Gatekeeper." In the 11+, you are looking for a common bond that connects four of the words, leaving one "intruder" behind. The secret weapon isn't reading—it's speed-sorting. If your child can find a shared property between just the first few words, they’ve already found the rule. The first word that snaps that pattern is the target. They don't need to process the whole line to get the points.

The Kitchen Cabinet "Apple, Banana, Carrot, Grape, Pear." (Finding the vegetable among the fruits).
The Metal Mine "Silver, Gold, Copper, Iron, Wood." (Spotting the non-metal).
The 11+ Nuance "Brave, Bold, Fearless, Reckless, Courageous." (Comparing positive traits vs. a negative one).

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Logic Sieve. Watch how we establish a "Majority Rule" one word at a time. Notice that the moment we hit the 2 vs. 1 threshold, the answer is mathematically locked. Any words remaining behind the "?" masks are just distractions that cost time. Click "Scan Next Word" to see the sieve in action.

Phase 3: The Engine

Move the outlier to different positions. Help your child notice that no matter where the "intruder" is hiding, we can usually stop thinking before we reach the fifth box. This Stop-Point Logic is the difference between finishing the paper and running out of time.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Linear Scan" Trap: Most children read all 5 words as a single list before they even start thinking about categories. This "storytime" approach wastes 5 to 10 seconds per question on information that doesn't change the outcome.

The Insider Fix: The "2 + 1" Rule. Tell your child: "The answer is revealed at word three." If Word 1 and 2 match, the rule is set. If Word 3 is different, that's the answer—stop reading! If Word 1 and 2 are different, Word 3 is the tie-breaker. The moment you have two of a kind and one oddity, the logic is closed.
