Module 47: Valid Cube Nets

Preview Stage 1: Foundational Phase (Years 2–4)
Foundation Intro

The Sticky Tape Test

Imagine you have a flattened cardboard box. To make it a cube, you have to fold the edges until they touch. If two edges can be taped together without overlapping or leaving a gap, the net is Valid.

"Look at these two faces on the net. They look far apart, but when the box folds, they become neighbors! Click 'Apply Sticky Tape' to see the gold tape join them together."
Edges are separate in 2D. Ready to tape!
Foundation Progressing

The Leapfrog Bridge

The fastest way to solve cube nets is to find Opposite Pairs. In any straight line of three or more squares, the faces separated by exactly one jump will be opposites. They can never touch in 3D!

"Look at the long row of four squares. If we start at Face A, we 'leapfrog' over Face B to find Face C. A and C are opposites. Click 'Bridge the Opposites' to see the partners."
Straight lines hide the best clues.

Preview Stage 2: Supplemental Mastery (Years 4–6)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A cube net is a "Flattened Cardboard Box." Success depends on identifying Opposite Pairs. Since opposites can never touch and can never be seen together, finding them is the fastest way to eliminate decoy answers.

Standard Cross Identify the "Base" if "Top" is selected in a 1-4-1 net.
The T-Shape Find all three opposite pairs in a T-style layout.
Advanced "Z" Solve a "Z-Net" where squares are staggered.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Touching Corner" Trap: Children assume squares sharing a corner on flat paper must touch on the cube. **Illusion!** Many distant faces meet at the same edge in 3D.

The Fix: The "Opposite Shield." Find the three opposite pairs. If an answer shows two opposites sharing a line, cross it out immediately.
