Module 56: Cloze (Contextual)

Foundation Intro

The Puzzle Piece Test

Choosing a word for a gap is like finding the right piece for a jigsaw puzzle. You can't just pick any piece—it has to match the "picture" (the meaning) of the words surrounding it.

"We have a sentence about a cold day. Look at the word **FREEZING**. Click 'Snap into Gap' to see it fly into place. Notice how the words 'Snow' and 'Ice' act like magnets—they pull the word 'Freezing' toward them because they belong together!"
The ? weather brought snow.
FREEZING
Only one word matches the "picture" of snow.
Foundation Progressing

The Logic Anchors

In the 11+, sentences have Anchors. These are specific words that force the answer to be one thing and not another. To win, you must find at least two anchors for every gap.

"Read this: 'The hiker felt ______ as his **water bottle** was **completely empty**.' Click 'Highlight Anchors'. See how those two clues point straight to the word **PARCHED**? Without those anchors, the hiker could have been happy or sad, but the anchors prove he is thirsty!"
"The hiker felt ________ after the climb, as his water bottle was completely empty."
Anchors provide the proof for your choice.

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of contextual cloze as "Semantic Radar." Success isn't about guessing; it's about reading past the hole to find the resolution. Magnet Words are specific clues that physically pull a vocabulary match toward the gap. If a sentence mentions "arid" and "sand," the word is mathematically forced to be parched or desert.

The Content Cat "...______ as it curled by the warm fire." Magnets: warm fire / curled. (Purred).
The Hungry Hiker "...so ______ he ate his dinner in seconds." Magnets: ate / seconds. (Famished).
11+ Politician "...______ speech failed to convince." Magnets: failed / skeptical. (Vague).

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Context Radar. Magnet Words before and after a gap create a narrow logic-window. You must find the only word in the bank that satisfies both anchors. One match is a guess; two matches is a lock.

Phase 3: The Engine

Load a scenario. The sentence is a set of scales; the correct word must balance the logic on both sides. Watch the Trace Log verify the Anchor Alignment.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Sounds Right" Trap: Children often choose a word because it fits a common phrase, even if it contradicts the clues. (e.g. picking "happy" because it's a common word, ignoring that the character is lost).

The Fix: The "Evidence Check." "No word is an island." Before committing, point to at least two Magnets that support the choice. If you can only find one, it's a distractor. **Evidence beats feeling.**
