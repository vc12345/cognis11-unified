The Decimal Mirage

The biggest trick in the 11+ is 1.5 hours. Your brain wants to say "1 hour and 50 minutes." But look at the clock! $.5$ means "Half a tank." In a 60-minute tank, half is 30 minutes, not 50.

"Move the slider to 0.5. Look at the gold hand on the clock. It’s pointing straight down at 30 minutes. $.5$ is just a fancy way of saying 'Half an Hour'. Don't let the decimal point trick you into thinking of 100!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+, time is where the decimal system fails. We live in Base-60. The secret is managing the "Overflow Point." Once a group of minutes hits 60, it must be packaged into a single hour unit. Success comes from knowing how to "Rewind" or "Fast-Forward" through these 60-minute blocks.

The Baker "A cake is in for 85 minutes. How many hours and minutes is that?"
The Traveler "A flight takes 1 hour and 45 minutes. Find the total minutes."
The Marathon "A runner finishes in 215 minutes. Convert to Hours and Minutes."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Decimal Mirage": Children see 1.5 hours and think 1h 50m. They are using the metric system on a clock!

The Fix: The "Half-Tank" Rule. Time doesn't speak metric. Since the container is 60 minutes wide, 0.5 (half) is always 30 minutes. If they use 100 as the base, the Saboteur wins.
