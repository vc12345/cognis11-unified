Module 1: The Unitary Method

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "GPS" for scaling problems. Instead of guessing how two groups of numbers relate, we always travel through a single point: The Value of One. Help your child master the two-step journey: Shrink the known total to find exactly one unit, then Grow that unit to hit your target. This "Unit Bridge" makes even the messiest numbers feel manageable.

The Shopping Trip "If 4 apples cost 80p, how much do 6 apples cost?"
The Road Trip "A car travels 150 miles on 5 gallons. How far on 8 gallons?"
The Heavy Lifting "9 identical crates weigh 108kg. What is the mass of 13 crates?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Backwards Divide": Bright children often try to divide the smaller number by the bigger one (e.g., $5 \div 20$) simply because it feels "easier" to fit them together. This leads to a messy decimal that makes no sense.

The Fix: Use the "Money is Boss" rule. Tell your child: "In 99% of these problems, the total value (like money or weight) goes into the calculation first." Always divide the Total Value by the Quantity to find your unit cost.
