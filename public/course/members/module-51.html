Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

Approach block-stacking challenges by looking for the **Hidden Floor Layers**. Gravity is a fixed rule. If a block sits exposed on layer three, two structural support blocks *must* sit hidden inside the layout below it to support the weight. Scoring high marks on non-verbal papers isn't about counting visible outer wall faces—it is about correctly calculating the total hidden volume required to hold the columns up.

The Level-2 Corner Stack: Look closely at a stacked layout. Make sure your child counts the hidden shared support blocks that are fully covered by front rows!
The Three-Tier Pillar: A single column stands 3 blocks high. How many background foundation blocks hide directly behind other units to support that top block?
The Asymmetric Grid: Find the exact block total of an uneven stack by scanning it column-by-column from back to front.

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Visible Side-Face" Overcounting Trap

When working through block questions quickly, children often fall into the trap of counting every single visible flat face they see. A single block sticking out at a corner exposes 3 separate faces at once (its Top face, Left face, and Right face). Counting every face will create a massive overcount score!

The Easy Fix: Count Only the Rooftops. Train your student with this shortcut rule: "Isolate only the flat roof diamonds." Pick one top roof face. Ask clearly: "How many blocks are stacked in this exact pillar?" Write that height number directly inside that roof space on the sheet. Adding up the pillar numbers from the roof entirely prevents double-counting!
