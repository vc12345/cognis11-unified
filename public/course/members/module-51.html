Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

Approach block-stacking challenges by searching out "Invisible Foundations." Gravity is a fixed physical constraint. If a target cube sits exposed on layer three, two structural blocks must reside hidden inside the layout below it. Winning non-verbal reasoning marks isn't about haphazardly counting visible surface panel faces—it is about correctly deducing the total hidden volume required to sustain the building framework.

The Level-2 L-Stack: Audit a stacked block array. Ensure your student accounts for the shared corner support element tucked out of view!
The Three-Tier Column: A standalone pillar rises 3 blocks high. How many background foundation blocks hide directly behind neighboring units to brace the apex?
The Irregular Skyline: Calculate the exact structural volume of an asymmetrical cube matrix packed with hidden baseline columns.

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Surface Face-Value" Overcounting Trap

When themes work quickly, they frequently fall victim to counting every single visible diamond face panel they spot. An individual corner block sticking out from the stack exposes 3 separate face directions simultaneously (its Top, its Left, and its Right). Tallying every face creates a massive overcount!

The Easy Fix: Practice the Rooftop Floor Tally Method. Train your student with this shortcut rule: "Isolate only the topmost roof diamonds." Select one golden top face. Ask explicitly: "Which floor level does this specific column rise to?" Write that height integer directly over the diamond face. Summing up the floor numbers of the roofs completely prevents double-counting.
