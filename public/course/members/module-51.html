Module 51: Block Counting

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "Invisible Foundations." In the 11+, you are shown a 3D stack of blocks and asked: "How many blocks are here total?" The secret weapon is realizing that Gravity never sleeps. If you see a block sitting on the 3rd floor, there must be two blocks hiding directly beneath it to hold it up. Success isn't about counting what you see—it's about deducing what must be there for the tower to stand.

The Simple L-Stack "Count the blocks in a 2-level stack. Don't forget the one hiding in the corner!"
The 3x3 Grid "A corner column is 3 blocks high. How many 'ghost' blocks are supporting the top one?"
The Skyline Challenge "Calculate the total volume of an irregular building where some columns are completely out of sight."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Face-Value" Trap: Most children count visible "diamond" faces. Because a single corner block on the top level shows 3 different faces (Top, Left, and Right), they often count that one block three times! This leads to a massive overcount.

The Insider Fix: The "Roof Tally" Rule. Tell your child: "Only count the roofs." They should pick one top-facing gold diamond and ask: "What floor is this on?" If it's on floor 4, that counts as 4 blocks. They should write "4" on that diamond, then move to the next gold roof. Summing the floor numbers of the roofs is the only way to never double-count.
