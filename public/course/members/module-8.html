Module 8: Fractions of Amounts

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of a fraction as two simple instructions: Slice and Collect. The bottom number tells you how many equal slices to cut your total into. The top number tells you how many of those slices to put on your plate. To solve any 11+ fraction problem, we always find the "weight" of just one slice first.

The Sweet Shop "Find 1/2 of 12 sweets. How many go into each of the two groups?"
The Birthday Cake "A cake weighs 500g and is cut into 5 slices. If 3 are eaten, how much did they weigh?"
The Long Run "A 40km race is 5/8 complete. How many km are left? (Hint: Find the 3/8 left over)."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Cutter and the Plate. Point to the bar below. The lines show where the "Cutter" (the bottom number) has sliced the total into equal boxes. Each box—our Golden Unit—holds the same value. To find that value, we divide the grand total by the number of boxes.

Phase 3: The Engine

Move the sliders with your child and watch the "Cutter" (Denominator) change the size of the boxes. Then, use the "Collector" (Numerator) to grab the boxes you want. Notice we always divide to find one box before we multiply to get the total.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Upside-Down" Mistake: Children often try to divide the total by the top number because it's usually the first one they see in the sentence. This leads to massive, confusing numbers.

The Fix: The "Knife and Hand" Rule. Remind your child: "The bottom number is the Knife; it cuts the total into pieces. The top number is the Hand; it picks them up." We always Divide by the Bottom and Multiply by the Top. If the answer looks bigger than the starting total, the fraction has gone upside down!
