Module 44: Layering (Z-Order)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Pancake Rule." In Non-Verbal Reasoning, examiners overlap shapes to test if your child can tell which was "dropped" onto the page last. The secret weapon is Perimeter Continuity. If a shape has a perfect, unbroken border, it must be on top. If a shape's border is "cut" or interrupted by another line, it is physically underneath. Mastering this allows your child to peel back the layers of any diagram in their mind.

The Simple Overlap "A red circle sits on a blue square. If the circle is whole but the square is cut, which is on top?"
The 3-Layer Stack "Look at three overlapping shapes. Rank them from the 'Bottom of the Pile' to the 'Top of the Pile'."
The Hidden Fragment "Identify the middle shape—the one that is partially covered by two others, leaving only a sliver visible."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Same Color" Trap: If two shapes are the same color or have very thin borders, your child's brain might merge them into one weird, giant shape. This makes them miss the fact that one is actually hiding behind the other.

The Insider Fix: The "T-Junction" Hunt. Tell your child: "Look for the Ts." They should ignore the big blobs of color and look only at where lines meet. If Shape A's line stops suddenly at Shape B's edge (making a 'T' shape), Shape A is the loser—it’s at the back. The shape that never stops its lines is the one on top.
