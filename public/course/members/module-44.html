The T-Junction Rule

When shapes overlap on a test page, their lines collide to form a visual letter T. Think of it like a mini traffic stop: the smooth crossbar line at the top of the T belongs to the front shape. The vertical stem line that crashes into it and stops dead belongs to the shape hiding underneath!

"Look at where the square and circle cross paths. Right now, it's hard to tell who is on top. Click 'Spot the T-Junctions' below. Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

Think of layer depth identification as mastering "The Pancake Rule." Non-verbal exam designers intentionally merge geometric elements to see if your student can isolate the "last dropped" item. The ultimate shortcut here is verifying Border Continuity. If an item maintains a totally uncompromised border, it owns the top slot. If its border is interrupted, it resides on a deeper layer. Train your child to peel back the layout layers by scanning for blocked lines.

The Overlapping Pair: "A red circle overlaps a blue square. The circle's edge is perfect, while the square is interrupted. Which sits on top?"
The Three-Layer Pile: "An overlapping trio of tokens. Rank their order from 'Deepest Layer' to 'Top Layer'."
The Fragmented Sliver: "Identify a hidden center shape that is heavily covered by two other items, leaving only a tiny visible sliver."

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Shared Color" Camouflage Trap

When two overlapping shapes happen to share the exact same color fill, a child's brain automatically blends them into a single blob. They fail to notice that one shape is actually lurking behind the other.

The Easy Fix: Isolate the Line Intersections. Coach your child with this method: "Look for where the borders collide." Ignore the color fills entirely and follow the outline tracks. If Shape A's line breaks off suddenly against Shape B's rim (creating that classic 'T' stop), Shape A is trapped at the back. The true top shape never breaks its line.
