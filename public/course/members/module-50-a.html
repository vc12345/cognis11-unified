Module 50: Paper Folding

Foundation Intro

The Wet Ink Test

Imagine you have a drawing in wet ink. If you fold the paper, the ink "stamps" onto the other side. Paper folding is just a Stamp and Mirror game. When you unfold, the original hole always creates a twin.

"Look at the blue dot. It's our 'Wet Ink'. Click 'Unfold' to see the paper flatten. Notice that the new dot isn't just in the same place—it's been flipped over the crease like a mirror image!"

The Crease Magnet

A fold acts like a magnet. If a hole is Close to the fold, its twin will be Close on the other side. If it is Far, the twin is Far. The distance never changes!

"Move the dot. See how the twin follows it? If the 'Real' hole moves toward the red crease, the 'Ghost' twin moves toward it too. This is the **Distance Constant**. Distance to the crease is the only truth in paper folding!"
Mirror the distance, not the position!

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "Mental Mirroring." Success comes from unfolding the paper in reverse. Every crease line is a perfect mirror. If there’s a hole on the left of a fold, there must be an identical twin on the right. Trace these reflections step-by-step until the paper is flat.

Single Split "1 Fold. 1 Punch. Trace symmetry across vertical center."
Quarter Fold "2 Folds. 2 Punches. Mirror across X and Y axes (8 holes total)."
11+ Master "3 Folds. Analyze radial reflections where holes seem to rotate."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Slide" Fallacy: Children often think a hole simply "slides" to the other side while staying in the same relative spot (e.g. top-left corner). **Critical Error.**

The Fix: The "Distance Constant." "The crease is a magnet." If a hole is 10mm from the fold, its twin **must** be 10mm from the fold on the other side. **Mirror the distance, not the spot.**
