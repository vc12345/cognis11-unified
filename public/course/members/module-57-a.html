Module 57: Cloze (Grammatical)

Foundation Intro

The Word Sorter

A sentence is like a machine with different shaped holes. A **Noun** (Person/Place/Thing) is a square. A **Verb** (Action) is a triangle. You can't put a triangle in a square hole! Grammatical Cloze is just finding the right shape for the gap.

"Look at our sentence: 'The ______ ran.' The hole is triangular—it needs an action! Click 'Jump' to see if it fits the Verb slot. Notice that 'Table' (a square noun) just won't work there because it's the wrong part of speech."

Foundation Progressing

The Robot Proxy

If you don't know which word fits a gap, use a **Robot Proxy**. Try saying "Thingy" (for Nouns) or "Doing-it" (for Verbs). If "Thingy" sounds right in the sentence, you are looking for a Noun in your word bank!

"Sentence: 'The ______ was very tall.' Let's try our proxies. 'The **Doing-it** was very tall'? No! 'The **Thingy** was very tall'? Yes! That sounds correct. Now we know for sure the missing word is a Noun."

"The ______ was very tall."

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "Sentence Engineering." Grammatical cloze isn't about the story—it's about the Syntactic Skeleton. Identify the "Job" the missing word is hired to do. Core pillars (Nouns/Verbs) hold the weight, while expansion modules (Adjectives/Adverbs) add data.

Noun Detailer "The ______ cat slept." Slot: Adjective (Info about cat).
Action Engine "He ______ through the park." Slot: Verb (Primary action).
Verb Detailer "The plane landed ______." Slot: Adverb (Info about landing).

Phase 3: The Engine

Load a blueprint. Identify the missing "Word Class." Remember: if a word ends in -ly, it's usually an Adverb; if it ends in -ed, it's likely a Verb.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Cool Story" Trap: Picking a word that sounds exciting but is grammatically broken. (e.g. "The *Bravely* explorer").

The Fix: The "Substitution Test." "Look at the neighbors." If the gap is between 'The' and a 'Noun', it **must** be an Adjective. Try swapping in "Big" or "Red." If they fit, the grammar is set.
