The Ingredient Jar

The Tally Stock-Take

To solve tricky 11+ anagrams, don't try to read the words. Instead, count the letters like a shopkeeper. If the tallies match perfectly, they are anagrams. If even one letter is missing, the bridge is broken.

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of an anagram as a "Re-boxed Recipe." An anagram is just a different arrangement of the exact same "stock." To find the truth, look past the sound of the word and perform a Character Inventory. Two words are only anagrams if their "Digital Fingerprints" match letter-for-letter.

Direct Check "Are 'ACT' and 'CAT' anagrams? Count the stock in both."
Scrambled Synonym "Rearrange 'LISTEN' to find a synonym for 'QUIET'."
The Impostor "Which is NOT an anagram of the others: RETAIN, REINA, TERNIA?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Scramble Chamber. No matter how much you shake these tiles, the "Stock" never changes. Anagrams are simply different ways to display the same pile of letters. Shake the chamber to see the arrangement shift.

Phase 3: The Engine

The Diagnostic Scan checks every single letter. Notice how the "Digital Fingerprint" must match perfectly for a valid result. Try the DESSERT vs DESERT test!

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One Letter Off" Trap: Most children see DESSERT and DESERT and assume they match because they "look" identical. The brain gets lazy!

The Fix: The "Alphabetical Stock-Take." "Don't trust your eyes; trust the count." Mentally sort the letters. [D-E-E-R-S-S-T] vs [D-E-E-R-S-T]. The missing 'S' becomes obvious.
