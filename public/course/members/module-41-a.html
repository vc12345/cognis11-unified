Foundation Intro

The Line-Up

Logical ordering is just like lining up for a photo. You are given a rule, and you have to move the items until that rule is happy. It’s not about guessing; it’s about checking the evidence.

The Chain Link

To solve harder puzzles, you must link your clues together like a chain. If A is taller than B, and B is taller than C, then B is the bridge that connects A to C!

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of logical ordering as the "Detective's Filter." The secret weapon isn't guessing the order—it's eliminating the impossible. Treat every clue like a screen that blocks out the wrong answers. By the time you've used every filter, only one truth remains.

Pairs "A is taller than B. There are only two friends. Who is shortest?"
Trio Chain "B is taller than A, and C is taller than B. Arrange them shortest to tallest."
11+ Master "D is tallest. B is taller than A but shorter than C. Find the sequence."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "First Clue" Trap: Children see "A is taller than B" and put A in the first position immediately. **Disaster!** A > B doesn't mean A is the overall tallest.

The Fix: The "Chain Link" Rule. "Don't build until you have all bricks." Link clues into a chain (A>B + B>C = A>B>C) first. **Link clues, then label spots.**
