Module 14: Interval Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "Hidden Space" secret. In the 11+, examiners love to trick children with sequences like clock strikes or fence posts. Bright children usually count the physical objects, but the real math always happens in the gaps between them. If your child can learn to ignore the objects and count the spaces, they’ll dodge the "Off-by-One" trap that catches most students out.

The Striking Clock "A clock strikes 4 times. If there are 3 seconds between each strike, how much time passes from the first strike to the last?"
The Garden Fence "A 30m straight fence has posts every 6m. How many posts are needed in total?"
The Doctor's Orders "A patient takes 6 doses of medicine, one every 45 minutes. How many minutes pass between the very first and the very last dose?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Look at the Boundary Map below. Point to the dark blue posts and the light blue gaps with your child. Notice that no matter how many posts you place, the number of gaps is always exactly one less. If you have 4 posts, you only have 3 gaps. This is the "Skeleton" of the problem—the gaps are where the distance and time actually live.

Phase 3: The Engine

Use the 'Number of Objects' box to add or remove posts. Watch the Hidden Gaps count change in the sky-blue pill. Help your child notice that the total distance is shared across the **gaps**, not the posts themselves. This is why we always subtract one before we do the math.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Fence Post" Trap: Most kids see "20m distance, 5m gaps" and say "4 posts." They are dividing the distance by the gap size and forgetting that a fence needs a post at the very start (the "Zero" mark) to hold it up!

The Insider Fix: The "Starting Anchor" Rule. Remind your child: "Your division answer tells you the number of Gaps. To find the number of Posts, you must always add the first anchor." Just remember the secret code: Gaps + 1 = Objects.
