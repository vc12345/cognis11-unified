The Crossroad Check

Sometimes a letter works for one word but fails for the others. These are Decoys. To find the true answer, you must mentally "triangulate"—checking that your chosen letter makes sense for every single path.

"Look at the crossroad. If we put 'O' in the middle, we get 'STOP' (Good!) and 'SICK' becomes... 'SIOK'? That doesn't sound right! Click 'Test T' to see the letter that actually works for both paths."
Is 'T' the true Master Key?

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Keyhole Rule." You aren't looking for three separate answers; you are looking for the only letter in the alphabet that completes three different spelling rules simultaneously. If it doesn't work for the whole set, it's a decoy designed to steal your time.

Middle Vowel "Find the letter for: B_RD, C_RD, H_RD."
Consonant Blend "Find the letter for: S_AMP, S_ICK, S_OP."
11+ Prefix "Find the letter for: _RAIN, _RACK, _RICK."

Phase 3: The Engine

Choose a word set and test different keys. Watch the Trace Log validate the logic. Help your child spot letters that are "Almost Right" (working for 2 out of 3).

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "First Word" Trap: Children find a letter that works for the first word and immediately circle it. They don't check if it turns the rest into gibberish.

The Fix: The "Total Validation" Rule. "Test the whole set." A letter is just a guess until it has been checked against every word in the cluster. **The answer must be a triple-threat.**
