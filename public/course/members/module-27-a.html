Module 27: Angle Logic

Foundation Intro

The Scissor Twins

When two lines cross, they create a "mirror" effect. If the top opening of the scissors gets wider, the bottom opening must get wider too. We call these Vertically Opposite Angles, and they are always identical twins.

"Look at the scissors. Move the slider to open them. Notice that as the top angle grows, the bottom one copies it perfectly. They are locked together. If one is **40°**, its twin across the pivot must be **40°** too!"
Open the scissors to see the twins!
Foundation Progressing

The Rubber Stamp

If two lines are Parallel (like train tracks), a line cutting through them creates two identical intersections. You can think of the top intersection as a rubber stamp that gets pressed down onto the bottom line. The angles don't change—they just move!

"We have a **60°** angle on the top track. Because the tracks are parallel, we can 'stamp' that angle down to the bottom track. Click 'Stamp' to see the angle slide down. These are called **Corresponding Angles**."
Ready to copy the pattern.

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of angles on parallel lines as "The Shadow Rule." In the 11+, you only ever have two numbers to deal with. Every angle in a parallel diagram belongs to one of two "families." They are either identical twins, or they add up to exactly **180°** to make a straight line.

Straight Line "Angles on a flat road add to **180°**. If one side is **110°**, what is its neighbor?"
The 'Z' Switch "A slope cuts tracks. If top-right is **60°**, what is its 'Alternate' (Z) partner?"
The 'C' Squeeze "If one co-interior angle is **75°**, why must the other be **105°**?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Transversal Pivot. The eight different angles are actually just two values repeated. They alternate like a pattern. Rotate the road to see how the "Acute Family" and "Obtuse Family" swap sizes.

Phase 3: The Engine

Rotate the slope. Notice that no matter how steep it gets, the Gold (Acute) and Blue (Obtuse) numbers always combine to make **180°**.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Looks Parallel" Trap: Children see two lines that "look" like tracks and use Z/F rules. **Trap!** If the lines aren't perfectly parallel, the top track has no connection to the bottom track.

The Fix: The "Arrow Hunt." "The math is illegal without the arrows." Look for the arrowheads or the words "Parallel Lines." **No arrows = No jumping.**
