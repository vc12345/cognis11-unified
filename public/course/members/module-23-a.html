Module 23: The Average Family

Foundation Intro

The Fair Share (Mean)

Imagine three friends have different amounts of candy. The Mean is the number everyone gets if they put all their candy in one big pile and share it back out perfectly fairly.

"Look at the piles: 5, 1, and 6. That's 12 candies in total. Click 'Fair Share' and watch the candies move. Since we have 3 friends, we shared the 12 candies so everyone has 4. That 4 is the Mean!"
Piles: 5, 1, and 6. Not fair yet!
Foundation Progressing

The Height Parade (Median)

The Median is simply the person in the middle of a line. But there's a rule: they must be standing in height order! If the numbers are jumbled, the person in the middle isn't the true Median.

"Look at these heights: 8, 2, 5, 9, 3. Who is in the middle? It looks like 5. But wait! Click 'Height Order'. Now that they are lined up correctly, you can see the true middle person is actually 5. Sometimes jumbled lists trick you!"
Heights: 8, 2, 5, 9, 3. Stand in order to find the middle!

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of averages as finding the "Center of Gravity" for a group. In the 11+, there are three siblings in the family: the Mean (the fair sharer), the Median (the middle of the queue), and the Mode (the popularity winner). Success depends on knowing which tool to use for the story provided.

The Mean "Find the mean of 3, 5, and 7. If shared equally, what is the score?"
The Median "Find the median of 15, 4, 9, 15, 2. Who is in the exact middle?"
The Missing "Mean age of four kids is 12. Three are 10, 11, 14. How old is the fourth?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Jumbled Median" Trap: Children look at a list like "8, 2, 5" and pick 2 because it's in the middle of the sentence. Disaster! The Median only counts if the list is sorted.

The Fix: The "Parade" Rule. "Numbers must stand in height order." Never find a Median until the numbers are Smallest to Largest. If there is an even number of items, find the tiny mean between the two central people.
