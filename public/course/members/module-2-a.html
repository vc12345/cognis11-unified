Foundation Intro

The Sweet Share

A ratio isn't just a list of numbers; it's a Sharing Rule. If the rule is 2:1, it means every single time you give 2 sweets to the blue plate, you must give 1 sweet to the gold plate.

"We have a pile of sweets and a sharing rule. Watch how many sets we can deal before the pile is empty! Every 'round' uses up a set of sweets from the pile."

The Magic Envelope

To share a large number like £100, we don't deal individual coins. We find the Value of One Envelope. Once you know what's inside one envelope, you just hand them out to match the ratio.

"Look at these envelopes. The ratio 3:2 means we have 5 envelopes in total. If we split the whole £100 into those 5 envelopes, how much goes in each one?"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

The secret weapon for every ratio problem is finding the Value of One Chunk. Once your child knows what one single piece is worth, they can hand out the right amount to anyone.

Level 1 Share £20 in the ratio 3:1. How much is the larger share?
Level 2 A 40cm rope is cut 5:3. How much longer is the first piece?
Level 3 A map scale is 2:7. If the total distance is 180km, find the smaller leg.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Sided" Mistake: Bright children often divide the total by just one side of the ratio. They forget that the total covers the entire bar.

The Fix: The "Add First" Rule. Always ask: "How many chunks are in the Whole Bar?" Add A + B first.
