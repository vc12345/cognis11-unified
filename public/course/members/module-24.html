Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

Think of an average as identifying a perfect "Fair Share" balanced height. In competitive 11+ exams, you will frequently be asked: "What value must the next participant score to shift the overall group average?" The master shortcut to solving this lies in evaluating the Grand Shared Pile. To track down the value of the missing person, directly compare the combined pile you hold right now against the total pile size you need to successfully hit the target benchmark. The bridge value is the empty gap separating these two grand totals.

The Missing Element: "The fair share value of 3 mystery numbers is exactly 5. Two of the numbers are confirmed to be 4 and 6. What is the value of the 3rd number?"
The Exam Score Challenge: "A student records initial test scores of 12, 15, and 10. What score must they achieve on their 4th test to pull their overall fair share average up to 13?"
The Group Weight Shift: "A group of 5 children weighs an average of 40kg. A 6th child joins the scale, and the new fair share average climbs to 41kg. Uncover the exact weight of the 6th child."

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Average of Averages" Illusion Trap: Rushed children frequently look only at the standalone average figures themselves. If their initial score average sits at 10 and the question says they want to reach 13, they will incorrectly assume the next single score just needs to be a 13. Stop right there! The next entry must be large enough to lift the *entire* historical group up to the new height level.

The Easy Fix: The "Total Volume" Rule. Remind your child: "The standalone average is just a mask; the combined total pile holds the true value." Always calculate the New Combined Total (New Group Count × Target Average) and subtract your Old Combined Total. The difference reveals the exact value of the missing entry. Train your child to discover the grand total values first.
