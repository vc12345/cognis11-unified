Module 29: Alpha-Numeric Decoder

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as the "Secret Agent Rule." In the 11+, examiners love to hide messages by swapping letters for numbers. Whether they use a simple count ($A=1$), a reverse countdown ($A=26$), or a shifted code ($A=3$), the secret weapon is Mapping Consistency. Once your child identifies the "Key" (which letter equals which number), they must apply that exact same logic to every letter in the word without breaking the pattern.

The Direct Code "If $A=1$ and $B=2$, what is the numerical code for the word 'FISH'?"
The Reverse Riddle "If the alphabet is flipped so $A=26$ and $Z=1$, what is the code for 'CAT'?"
The 11+ Shift "A secret code uses a $+3$ offset ($A=4$). Decode the numbers '4-18-21-13' to find the word."

Help your child notice how the whole alphabet moves together like a locked gears—if you know one pair, you know them all.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Alphabet Wall" Trap: When a shift moves past Z or before A, children often freeze. If the code is $A=24$, and they need to find the value of $C$, they think the alphabet has "run out" of room.

The Insider Fix: The "Alphabet Clock" Rule. Tell your child: "The alphabet is a circle, not a line." Just like a clock goes from 12 back to 1, the alphabet goes from Z back to A. If they hit the "Wall" at Z, they just keep counting from 1 again at A. There is no end to the loop.
