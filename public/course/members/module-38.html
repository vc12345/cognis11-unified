Module 38: Synonym Precision

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "Vocabulary Volume." In the 11+, synonyms aren't just words that mean the same thing—they are words that carry different Levels of Intensity. A slight breeze is gentle, but a hurricane is violent. The secret weapon is matching the "Magnitude" of the word to the drama of the sentence. If your child can learn to rank synonyms from quiet to loud, they’ll dodge the decoys that are technically correct but "too weak" for the story.

The Heat Check "Which word is 'louder': Warm or Scorching?"
The Light Scale "Rank these from weakest to strongest: Bright, Dim, Dazzling."
The 11+ Nuance "Contrast the shades of Thrifty (Careful) vs. Stingy (Extreme)."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Volume Mismatch" Trap: Most children choose a synonym that is accurate but "underpowered" for the sentence. For example, describing a massive volcanic explosion as a "noisy" event instead of a "cataclysmic" one. The word fits, but the drama is missing.

The Insider Fix: The "Drama Test." Tell your child: "Check the neighbors." Look at the other verbs and adjectives in the sentence. If they use high-drama words like shattered, shrieked, or instantly, they must choose the synonym from the extreme red end of the spectrum. Match the drama level to find the answer.
