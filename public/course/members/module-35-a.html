Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as the "Expendable Letter" mystery. One letter migrates from the first word into the second. The secret is recognizing that the letter must leave a perfect word behind. It is a dual-validation task: the migration must be linguistically legal on both ends.

The Easy Swap "Move one letter from CHART to EAT."
The Hidden Slot "Move a letter from STEAM to BIT. Where does it land?"
11+ Powerplay "Find the letter migrating from POUND to EACH."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Sided" Trap: Children find a letter that makes a great new word in the second box (e.g. H from CHART makes HEAT) and stop. They forget to check if CART is a real word!

The Fix: The "Dual Dictionary" Rule. "The letter is a bridge." Both sides must stay standing. Perform two checks: 1. Is the first word alive? 2. Is the second word born? **Both must pass.**
