Module 27: Angle Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of angles on parallel lines as "The Shadow Rule." In the 11+, you’ll often see a single line (the transversal) cutting through a pair of parallel train tracks. The secret weapon is realizing that you only ever have two numbers to deal with. Every single angle in that messy diagram belongs to one of two "families." They are either identical twins, or they add up to exactly 180° to make a straight line.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Looks Parallel" Trap: Many children see two lines that "look" like tracks and immediately start using Z and F angle rules. This is a trap. If the lines aren't perfectly parallel, the angles at the top track have absolutely no connection to the ones at the bottom.

The Insider Fix: The "Arrow Hunt." Tell your child: "The math is illegal without the arrows." They must look for the little arrowheads printed on the lines or the words "Parallel Lines" in the text. If those aren't there, they can only solve angles on a single track, not jump between them. No arrows = No jumping.
