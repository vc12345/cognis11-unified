Module 41: Logical Ordering

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of logical ordering as the "Detective's Filter." In the 11+, you are given several clues about who is taller, heavier, or faster than someone else. The secret weapon isn't guessing the order—it's eliminating the impossible. Instead of trying to find the "winner" immediately, your child should treat every clue like a screen that blocks out the wrong answers. By the time they've used every clue, only one truth will remain.

The Simple Pair "A is taller than B. There are only two friends. Who is the shortest?"
The Trio Chain "B is taller than A, and C is taller than B. Arrange them from shortest to tallest."
The 11+ Challenge "D is the tallest. B is taller than A, but shorter than C. Find the full sequence."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "First Clue" Trap: Most children see the first clue (e.g., "A is taller than B") and immediately put A in the first position. Stop! This is a disaster. Just because A is taller than B doesn't mean A is the tallest person in the whole group.

The Insider Fix: The "Chain Link" Rule. Tell your child: "Don't build the house until you have all the bricks." They should link clues together into a chain (A > B and B > C becomes A > B > C). Only once every person is linked should they decide on the final order. Link the clues, then label the spots.
