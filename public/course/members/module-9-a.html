Module 9: Sequential Fractions

Foundation Intro

The Vanishing Pie

In these puzzles, the total "shrinks" halfway through. Once the first slice is gone, the next person only looks at what is **actually left** on the plate. It's like having two separate snacks in a row.

"We start with a whole pie. First, we take 1/2 of it. Now, look at what’s left. If we take 1/2 again, we aren't taking another big half; we are taking a half of that **small** leftover piece. Watch the pie get smaller and smaller!"
Foundation Progressing

The Zoom-In Bar

To solve these in your head, you must draw a "Stop Sign" after the first step. Once you subtract the first amount, that remainder becomes your brand new "Full Bar" for the second fraction.

"Look at the first bar. We spent some money. See those dotted lines? They zoom in on the remainder. The second bar only cares about that leftover space. We never go back to the original total!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "Remainder Trap." The "Total" changes halfway through. Once the first slice is gone, the next fraction only cares about what is left. If your child can focus strictly on the Remainder, they’ll dodge the most common 11+ mistake.

The Sweets "Sam has 20 sweets. He eats 4. He gives 1/2 of what is left to Sue. How many left?"
The Book "A 120-page book. Read 1/3 Monday, then 1/4 of the remainder Tuesday. Pages left?"
The Budget "£400. Spend 2/5 on a hotel and 1/3 of the remainder on travel. Final balance?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Watch the "pie" shrink. The First Cut finds the first amount. Then, the Remainder becomes the "new 100%" for Stage 2. We aren't cutting the original bar anymore; we are only cutting the piece that survived.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Original Total" Trap: Most kids try to calculate the second fraction using the original total (e.g., 2/5 of £600 instead of 2/5 of the leftover £450).

The Fix: Circle the Keyword. Look for "Remainder" or "Left". If they see it, they must draw a "Stop Sign" after Stage 1. They have to subtract to find the new pile before they touch the second fraction.
