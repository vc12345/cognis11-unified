Module 37: Missing Letters

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Keyhole Rule." In the 11+, your child will find puzzles where three different words are all missing the exact same letter. The secret weapon is Linguistic Triangulation. They aren't looking for three separate answers; they are looking for the only letter in the alphabet that is powerful enough to complete three different spelling rules at the same time. If it doesn't work for all three, it's a decoy.

The Middle Vowel "Find the letter that completes: B_RD, C_RD, H_RD."
The Consonant Blend "Find the letter that completes: S_AMP, S_ICK, S_OP."
The 11+ Prefix "Find the letter that completes: _RAIN, _RACK, _RICK."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Master Key. Type a single letter into the central gold keyhole. Point out to your child how the paths light up. If they type a letter that only completes one word, the other two paths remain broken. The answer is only correct when the whole system is "unlocked" and readable.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "First Word" Trap: Most children find a letter that works for the very first word and immediately circle it. For example, in RA_N, they see "RAN" and stop. They don't realize that the letter 'N' will make gibberish out of the other two words in the set.

The Insider Fix: The "Total Validation" Rule. Tell your child: "Test the whole set or the Saboteur wins." A letter is just a guess until it has been physically checked against all three words. If word number 3 doesn't make sense, they must throw that letter away and try the next one. The answer must be a triple-threat.
