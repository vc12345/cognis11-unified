Module 31: Vowel/Consonant Ciphers

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Sorting Office." In advanced coding, the rule depends on what kind of letter you are holding. Vowels (A, E, I, O, U) go through the blue door, and Consonants go through the red door. The secret weapon is realizing that each group has its own math. Your child must learn to act like a gatekeeper—categorizing every letter through a mental "check" before they even think about shifting it.

The Basic Gate "If Vowels shift +1 and Consonants stay the same, what is the code for 'BAT'?"
The Split Shift "Vowels shift +2, Consonants shift -1. Find the code for the word 'DOG'."
The 11+ Challenge "A word is encoded using split rules to become 'QMANFU'. Can you reverse the gates to find 'PLANET'?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Property Gatekeeper. Notice how the colors change based on the letter type. Blue tiles are for Vowels and Red tiles are for Consonants. Help your child see that the letter 'A' in the middle of a word might jump forward, while the 'B' next to it might jump backward. They aren't following one path; they are following two different sets of orders.

Phase 3: The Engine

Type a word and set separate shifts for Vowels and Consonants. Watch the Trace Log as it sorts each character. Ask your child to spot the moment the rule "flips"—usually when the word moves from a consonant to a vowel. This visual "tagging" is exactly how they should solve these in the exam.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Track" Trap: Most children get into a rhythm. If they shift the first two consonants by -1, their brain will go on autopilot and shift the vowel by -1 as well. They forget that the rule "re-boots" itself at every single letter.

The Insider Fix: The "Label Before You Leap" Rule. Tell your child: "Don't do the math yet." Before they shift anything, they should write a tiny 'V' or 'C' above every letter in the word. Only once the whole word is tagged should they pick up their pencil to do the jumping. Tagging stops the autopilot.
