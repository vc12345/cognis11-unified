Module 4: Flipping the Rules (Opposites)

Foundation Intro

The Tidy-Up Race

Usually in math, when one number goes up, the other number goes up too. But sometimes rules flip into opposites: adding more helpers makes the time go down. Think about packing away toys: if you work all by yourself, it takes a long time. But if a few friends join in to help, the job is finished in a flash!

"Look at our big messy pile of building blocks. If we have only 1 helper working alone, the clock shows it will take 12 long minutes. Let's add more friends to the screen and watch how quickly the timer drops!"

The Balancing Act

When rules flip like this, there is a secret Magic Number that stays exactly the same. It works just like a see-saw. If you pile more weight on the "Helpers" side, the "Time" side must instantly shrink to keep the see-saw perfectly balanced.

"If 1 helper takes 12 hours, our locked Magic Number is 12. Watch what happens if we slide the bar up to 4 helpers. The see-saw shifts because the time automatically drops down to 3 to keep the math balanced!"

Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

The secret weapon for tracking opposite rules is uncovering the Magic Number (the total size of the job), which never changes. Once your child multiplies the starting characters to find that master value, the final step is just one quick division away.

Starting Group × Starting Time = The Locked Magic Number
The Clean Up: "If 2 children take 6 minutes to put away the toys, how long will it take 3 children working at the same speed?"
The Building Site: "An engineering team of 4 builders takes 10 days to lay a track. How long would it take 5 builders?"
The Water Pumps: "6 water pumps take 14 hours to empty a tank completely. If 2 pumps break down, how long does the job take now?"

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "More Means More" Trap: Eager children often spot numbers changing and automatically assume that doubling the workforce will double the time taken. **This is an exam trap!**

The Easy Fix: The "Toy Test." Remind them: more friends back at home means less cleaning time. Always find the locked Magic Number first by multiplying, then **divide** it by the size of the new team.
