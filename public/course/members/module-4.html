Module 4: Inverse Proportion

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as the "See-Saw" Rule. Usually, in math, if one number goes up, the other follows. Inverse proportion is different: if you double the number of workers, you cut the time in half. The "Secret Weapon" is realizing that the Total Work (the Magic Number) never changes. To solve these, you just find that Magic Number first:

Workers × Time = The Magic Number
The Tidy-Up "If 2 children take 6 minutes to tidy a room, how long for 3 children?"
The Building Site "4 builders take 10 days to build a wall. How many days for 5 builders?"
The Water Pump "6 pumps empty a tank in 14 hours. 2 break down. How long for the rest?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "More is More" Trap: Bright children often go on autopilot and think: "If 4 workers take 10 days, then 8 workers must take 20 days." They've doubled the helpers but somehow made the job take twice as long!

The Fix: The "Lego Test." Ask your child: "If more friends come over to help you tidy your Lego, does it take you more time or less time?" Once they realize it's less, remind them that they must divide the Magic Number by the new helpers, not multiply.
