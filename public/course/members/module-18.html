Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

This rule is known as the "Hierarchy of Power." Signs possess internal energy rankings. High-energy actions (like Multiplication or Powers) will always override low-energy partners (like Addition and Subtraction), forcing them to wait. Never read a long math statement from left to right—read it like a high-priority checklist.

The Basic Trap: "$5 + 3 \times 2$. Does our addition step or our multiplication step claim first priority?"
The Shield Block: "$(15 - 5) \div 2 + 4$. The bracket rings form a protective shield around the subtraction sign, moving it straight to the front."
The Downward Path: "$10 + 2^3 \div (6 - 2)$. Map your route all the way from the top power value down to the base addition sign."

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Left-to-Right Bookworm" Trap: Children naturally read from left to right. When faced with a string like $10 - 2 \times 3$, they will almost always try to compute $10 - 2 = 8$ first. This runs straight into an exam trap!

The Easy Fix: The "Eyes Scan First" Rule. Tell your child: "Your pencil must stay perfectly still until your eyes have scanned the entire line." Always hunt down your heavyweights first. In the string $10 - 2 \times 3$, our multiplication sign is heavier. Compute $2 \times 3 = 6$ first. Then finish with $10 - 6 = 4$.
