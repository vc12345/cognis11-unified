Module 18: BODMAS Hierarchy

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "Hierarchy of Power." In the 11+, examiners love to set traps by putting addition at the start of a sentence and multiplication at the end. Your child's brain will want to solve it like a book—left to right. But in math, symbols have "energy levels." High-energy operations (Multiplication, Powers) will always "crush" low-energy ones (Addition, Subtraction) into waiting their turn.

The Left-Right Trap "Find the value of $5 + 3 \times 2$. Does the addition or the multiplication happen first?"
The Shielded Math "Solve $(15 - 5) \div 2 + 4$. Remember the brackets create a 'shield' around the subtraction."
The Energy Path "Calculate $10 + 2^3 \div (6 - 2)$. Trace the path from the 'Power' down to the 'Plus'."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Think of this as the Priority Sieve. When you throw an equation into the machine, the symbols get caught in different layers. The top layers are the "Heavyweights"—they must be dealt with before anything can fall through to the layers below. Point to the sieve: the symbols at the top have all the power.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Bookworm" Trap: Most children read an equation from left to right, just like a storybook. If they see $10 - 2 \times 3$, they will confidently do $10 - 2 = 8$ first, then $8 \times 3 = 24$. This is a disaster.

The Fix: The "Priority Scan." Tell your child: "The pen doesn't move until the eyes scan the line." They must hunt for the 'Heavyweights' first. In $10 - 2 \times 3$, the multiplication is heavier. It happens first: $2 \times 3 = 6$. Then, and only then, does the subtraction happen: $10 - 6 = 4$. Eyes first, pen second.
