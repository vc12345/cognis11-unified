Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as the "Word Hinge." You are looking for one single word that can glue two separate words together. This mystery word must act as the end of the first and the start of the second. The secret weapon is dual-scanning: the word must be strong enough to hold up both sides of the bridge simultaneously.

Weather Game "Find the word for: RAIN [ ____ ] BALL. It must join with both."
Link Challenge "Find the bridge: FOOT [ ____ ] ROOM. Both must be real compound nouns."
Sea Captain "Identify the bridge for SEA [ ____ ] SIDE. The hinge must click into place."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Half-Match" Trap: Children find a word that fits the first block (RAIN-COAT) and stop. They forget to check "COAT-BALL" (not a word!).

The Fix: The "Tug-of-War" Rule. "The bridge is pulled from both sides." Once you have a candidate, mentally pull it over to the second word. If it doesn't "click" instantly, it's a fake. **Both sides must be true.**
