Module 10: Constant Speed

Foundation Intro

The Snail Step

Speed isn't a scary math word; it's just the size of a Single Jump. If a snail has a speed of "3 cm per second," it means every time the clock ticks, that snail moves exactly 3 cm forward.

"Look at our snail. His speed is set to 2 cm per second. Every time you click 'Tick', he will take one jump. Count with me: One tick, 2 cm. Two ticks, 4 cm. Speed is just the distance of **one** jump!"

Foundation Progressing

The Distance Total

Distance is the Grand Total. If you know the Speed (how far in 1 hour) and the Time (how many hours you traveled), you just multiply them together. It's like building a long road out of identical speed blocks.

"We are driving at 60 mph. That's our block size. If we drive for 3 hours, we just put three 60-mile blocks together. See how the distance grows? Distance is the boss because it's the result of the other two!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of Speed, Distance, and Time as the "Travel Trio." They are three sides of the same story. The secret weapon is recognizing that Distance is the Boss—it sits at the top of everything. If you know how the trio connects, you can calculate any missing part of the journey in seconds.

The Toy Car "A remote car travels 20m in 4 seconds. What is its speed?"
The Fast Train "A train travels at 75mph. How far in 3 hours?"
The Flight "A plane flies 1,200 miles at 400mph. How long does it take?"

The Magic Triangle. This is your logical map. Tell your child: "Cover the letter we are looking for with your thumb." What's left tells you the math. Distance (D) is on top; Speed (S) and Time (T) are the partners at the bottom.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Clock Confuser": Bright children often forget that clocks and math don't speak the same language. For a 1 hour 30 minute trip, they often plug 1.3 into the formula. Trap!

The Fix: The "Divide by 60" Rule. Minutes are fractions of 60. 30 minutes is 0.5 (30/60). 15 minutes is 0.25. Always convert minutes to decimals before they touch the triangle!
