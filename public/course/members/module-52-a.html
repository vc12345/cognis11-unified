Module 52: Explicit Retrieval

Foundation Intro

The Highlighter Hunt

Explicit Retrieval is like a "Find the Detail" game. The answer isn't a secret—it's written right there on the paper. To find it, you have to ignore all the other words and hunt for the one thing the question asks for.

"Question: **What color was the cat's collar?** Let's look at the sentence. Click 'Highlight' to sweep the yellow pen across. Notice how our eyes stop the moment we see the color word 'Green'!"
The fluffy cat sat on the mat wearing a green collar.

Foundation Progressing

The Noun Magnet

To find an answer fast, don't read the whole story. Read the question first and find the **Hook** (the special name or object). Then, use your eyes like a magnet to find that same word in the text!

"Question: **What did Professor Snape find?** The Hook is 'Professor Snape'. Click the 'Hook' in the question, then click where Snape is hiding in the text. See how the Hook leads you straight to the answer?"
What did Professor Snape find? HOOK: PROFESSOR SNAPE
The hall was empty. Professor Snape walked slowly toward the desk. He reached down and picked up a silver key.
Identify the "Hook" noun to save time.

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of Explicit Retrieval as "Data Mining." Some answers aren't hidden behind clues—they are literally printed on the page. The secret weapon is the "Hook and Type" system. Program your brain with a specific **Noun** (the Hook) and a **Question Word** (the Type, like "Who" or "When") to filter the noise.

The Catcher "The cat wore a blue collar." Q: What color? (Target: Adjective)
The Runner "Snape left at midnight." Q: When? (Target: Time/Date)
The Link "Signed in Berlin." Q: Where? (Target: Place Name)

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Fact HUD. Program your query before looking at the text. Narrowing your vision tells the eyes to ignore filler words and hunt for specific data types (digits for "When", Capitals for "Who"). Extraction speed is the goal.

QUERY: "When was the base built?"
TYPE: WHEN (Numbers)
HOOK: BASE (Target)
SCAN STATUS: FILTERING FOR TEMPORAL DATA

Phase 3: The Engine

Choose a retrieval type to set the "Filter." Then, click the correct word in the passage to retrieve the data point.

In 1924, the Explorer Finch discovered the Emerald Idol inside a hidden cave.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Enjoyment" Trap: Most children read the entire story first because they find it interesting. This burns 3 minutes of clock time before they even see a question!

The Fix: The "Sacrificial Scan." "Don't read the story, solve the mystery." Read the question **first**. Identify the Hook and the Type. Use a finger to scan in a fast 'Z' shape, ignoring every word that doesn't match the target type.
