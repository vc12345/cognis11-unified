Module 50: Paper Folding

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "Mental Mirroring." In the 11+, you are shown a piece of paper being folded and then "punched" with a hole. The secret weapon is realizing that you aren't trying to guess where the holes land—you are unfolding the paper in reverse. Every crease line is a perfect mirror. If there’s a hole on the left of a fold, there must be an identical twin on the right. Your child’s job is to trace these reflections step-by-step until the paper is flat again.

The Single Split "1 Fold (Half). 1 Punch. Trace the symmetry across the vertical center to find the pair."
The Quarter Fold "2 Folds (Quarter). 2 Punches. Mirror the pattern across both the X and Y axes to find the group of 8."
The 11+ Masterclass "3 Folds (Complex). Analyze radial reflections where the hole positions seem to 'rotate' around the center."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Slide" Fallacy: Most children think the hole simply "moves" to the other side of the paper while staying in the same relative spot (e.g., always staying in the "top-left" corner of its section). This is a point-stealing trap.

The Insider Fix: The "Distance Constant." Tell your child: "The crease is a magnet." If a hole is 10mm away from the fold line, its mirror-twin must be exactly 10mm away on the other side. If the hole is near the edge, its twin is near the edge. Distance to the crease is the only thing that never lies. Mirror the distance, not the position.
