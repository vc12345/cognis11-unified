Module 33: Hidden Words

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Seam Secret." In standard reading, our eyes see spaces as walls that define where words begin and end. In the 11+, examiners love to "glue" a secret word across those gaps. To find it, your child must learn to dissolve those walls and treat the whole sentence as a continuous stream. Most hidden words are "Bridge Words"—they steal their letters from the end of one word and the start of the next.

The Atomic Bridge "Find the 3-letter word hidden in: 'THE ATOM'."
The Deep Forest "Find the 4-letter word hidden in: 'FISH ARE DEEP'."
The Foul Play "Find the 4-letter word hidden in: 'MADE A FOUL'."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Inside Job" Trap: Most children look for a small word entirely inside one of the big words (like finding "CAT" inside "SCATTER"). In the 11+, the hidden word is almost never inside a single word. If it were that easy, it wouldn't be a puzzle!

The Insider Fix: The "Pinch" Method. Tell your child: "The answer lives in the cracks." Have them place their finger over the space between two words. They should look at the 2 letters before the space and the 2 letters after the space. That junction is the 90% "Hot Zone" where the hidden word is hiding. Focus on the seams, ignore the centers.
