Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

Think of this verbal reasoning drill as cracking "The Junction Seam Secret." We naturally treat empty blank spaces like solid walls on a page. In highly competitive entrance exams, secret code words are instead "glued" directly across those structural gaps. We call these Bridge Words—they sneakily steal a couple of letters from the trailing end of one word and blend them with the starting characters of the next. To pull them out of hiding, learn to evaluate an entire puzzle phrase as one unbroken string of characters.

Safari Level 1: "Unearth the 3-letter hidden word camouflage resting inside: 'THE ATOM'."
Safari Level 2: "Track down the 4-letter hidden word camouflage anchored inside: 'FISH ARE DEEP'."
Safari Level 3: "Isolate the 4-letter hidden word camouflage hiding inside: 'MADE A FOUL'."

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Internal Nesting" Illusion Trap: Most children scan exclusively *inside* the borders of a single large word looking for an internal match (like spotting CAT inside the word SCATTER). In competitive 11+ papers, the target answer is almost never tucked inside a single standalone word.

The Easy Fix: The Finger Pinch Scanning Method. Coach your student with this golden rule: "The answer always breathes inside the cracks." Have them place a finger directly over the blank space mark on the page. Force them to scan only the 2 letters immediately before the space and the 2 letters immediately trailing it. That tight junction is the 90% Hot Zone. Ignore the centers of words; scan the seams.
