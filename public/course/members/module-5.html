Module 5: Direct Proportion

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Direct Proportion is just fancy talk for "Perfect Lockstep." If you buy twice as many stickers, you pay twice as much money. The easiest way to teach this is to find the Value of One first. If your child can find the "unit price," they can scale up to any number—whether it's 10 people or 10,000—without the math getting messy.

The Sticker Shop "If 2 stickers cost 10p, how much do 6 stickers cost?"
The Kitchen Scale "A recipe for 4 people uses 300g of sugar. How much sugar for 10 people?"
The Map Master "On a 1:25,000 scale map, an 8cm road represents a massive real-world distance. How many km is it?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Just Add a Bit" Trap: If a recipe for 4 people needs 10g of salt, and we scale it to 6 people, bright children often think: "That's 2 more people, so I'll just add 2g more salt." They are treating the recipe like a pile of blocks rather than a balanced mix.

The Fix: The "Taste Test." Ask them: "If we just add a tiny bit, will it still taste the same, or will it be too weak?" Remind them that proportion is a Multiplication Game, never an addition game. If the quantity doubles, the ingredient MUST double to keep the taste perfect.
