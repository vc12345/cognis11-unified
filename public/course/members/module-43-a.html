Module 43: Reflection Logic

Foundation Intro

The Wet Ink Fold

Imagine you have a piece of paper with a wet ink drawing on one side. If you fold the paper in half and press down, the ink transfers to the other side. This is a Reflection!

"Look at the heart on the left. Click 'Fold Paper'. Notice that the reflected heart isn't just sliding over; it is turning around to face the opposite way. In a reflection, the shape's 'face' always points back at the mirror!"
The original ink is on the left.
Foundation Progressing

The Distance Twins

Every point on a shape has a "Distance Twin" on the other side of the mirror. If a corner is 3 steps away from the mirror on the left, it must be exactly 3 steps away on the right.

"Look at the Blue Dot. It is far away from the mirror. Click 'Find Twin'. Notice how the Gold Dot appears at the exact same distance on the other side. This is called the **Equidistant Rule**. The mirror never lies about distance!"
Original point is 100 units from the mirror.

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of reflection as "The Mirror Trap." In the 11+, examiners use flips to hide patterns. The secret weapon is the Equidistant Rule: every point has a twin. If the "arms" of a shape point away from the mirror, the reflected arms must point away too. Mirrors reverse direction, but they preserve distance.

Vertical Flip "Reflect a square across a vertical line. Does the shape change?"
Asymmetry "Horizontal flip on a 'P' shape. Which way is the loop facing now?"
11+ Diagonal "Reflection across a 45° line. Where does the top corner land?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Mirror Axis. Pick a corner of the Gold 'F'. Count its distance to the red line. In a true reflection, it lands at the exact same distance on the other side. Track the furthest points first—they are the easiest to verify.

Phase 3: The Engine

Select an axis and click "Perform Flip." Notice how the "ghost" stays at the origin to highlight the transformation logic.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Slide" Trap: Many children think reflection is just a slide (translation). They move the shape without changing its "face." If the 'F' faces right on the left, they keep it facing right on the right.

The Fix: The "Corner Count." "The furthest stays furthest." If a tip is the furthest thing on the left, it must become the furthest on the right. **Count the gaps to prove the flip.**
