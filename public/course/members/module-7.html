Module 7: Reverse Percentages

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Reverse Percentages are about Time Travel. Usually, we start at the beginning and work forward to find a sale price. Here, we are standing at the "Destination" (the final price) and need to Rewind back to the original cost. Since multiplication moved us forward, we must use Division to drive the car back to the start.

The Toy Sale "A toy costs £5 in a half-price sale. What was it worth originally?"
The Sneaker Discount "After a 10% discount, trainers cost £45. Find the price before the sale."
The House Hike "A house worth £240,000 increased by 20%. What was the starting value?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Reverse Bridge. Look at the path below. To get to the "Final Value," we multiplied by a number. To get back to the "Original," we apply the Inverse Secret: we divide by that exact same multiplier. Point to the gold bubble—that's our shortcut home.

Phase 3: The Engine

Use the inputs to simulate a sale or a price hike. Help your child notice that the "Original" value always changes instantly when you Divide the final price. Watch how the numbers "stretch" back to their larger starting size.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Forward Percent" Trap: If a shirt is £90 after a 10% discount, your child will be tempted to find 10% of £90 (£9) and add it back to get £99. Stop! This is a trap. That 10% was originally stolen from a bigger number, not the £90.

The Fix: The "Division Stretch." Never calculate a percentage of the sale price. Always find the Multiplier first (like 0.90 for a 10% off sale) and then DIVIDE. Division is the only tool that can correctly "stretch" the price back to its true original size.
