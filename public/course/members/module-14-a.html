Foundation Intro

The Bell Ringer

If a clock strikes 3 times, you might think it takes 3 seconds. But the time only passes **between** the chimes. The first chime is the starting gun—it happens at "Zero." We only count the silence that follows!

"Click the bell 3 times. Watch the timeline. The first chime is our 'Start'. Notice that for 3 chimes, there are only 2 gaps of time. In math, the action happens in the gaps, not the noises!"

The Fence Builder

Building a straight fence is a classic 11+ trick. If you have 4 wooden panels, you need **5 posts**. Why? Because you need one at the very start and one at the very end. The number of objects is always one more than the number of spaces.

"We need to build a fence. Move the slider to add panels. Count the dark blue posts with your child. Notice the pattern? If we have 3 panels (gaps), we always need 4 posts to hold them up!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "Hidden Space" secret. In the 11+, sequences like clock strikes or fence posts are designed to trip you up. The real math always happens in the gaps between objects. To succeed, ignore the objects and count the spaces. You’ll dodge the "Off-by-One" trap every time.

The Clock "A clock strikes 4 times. 3 seconds between each strike. How much time from first to last?"
The Fence "A 30m straight fence has posts every 6m. How many posts are needed?"
The Medicine "A patient takes 6 doses, one every 45 mins. Total time from first to last?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Look at the Boundary Map. No matter how many posts you place, the number of gaps is always exactly one less. If you have 4 posts, you have 3 gaps. The distance and time live in the gaps, not the posts.

Objects: 4 Gaps: 3

Phase 3: The Engine

Adjust the number of objects. Watch how the Hidden Gaps count changes. The total distance is shared across the gaps, which is why we always subtract one before dividing.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Fence Post" Trap: Most kids see "20m distance, 5m gaps" and say "4 posts." They divide distance by gap size but forget the Zero Anchor at the start.

The Fix: The "Starting Anchor" Rule. Division tells you the number of Gaps. To find the number of Posts, you must always add the first anchor: Gaps + 1 = Objects.
