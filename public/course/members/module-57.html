Module 57: Cloze (Grammatical)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "Sentence Engineering." In the 11+, grammatical cloze tests aren't about the story—they are about the Syntactic Skeleton. Every sentence is a machine with specific slots. There is always a Noun (the subject) and a Verb (the action). You can add "Expansion Modules": Adjectives to describe the noun, or Adverbs to describe the action. To solve these, your child must identify the "Job" the missing word is being hired to do.

The Noun Detailer "The ______ cat slept."
Slot: Adjective (Info about the cat). Fit: Fluffy.
The Action Engine "He ______ through the park."
Slot: Verb (The primary action). Fit: Sprinted.
The Verb Detailer "The plane landed ______."
Slot: Adverb (Info about the landing). Fit: Smoothly.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Cool Story" Trap: Most children pick a word because it makes the story sound exciting, even if it’s grammatically broken. For example, picking "Bravely" for the gap in "The _____ explorer" because explorers are brave. Even though it "feels" right, "Bravely" is an adverb and physically cannot describe a noun.

The Insider Fix: The "Substitution Test." Tell your child: "Look at the neighbors." If the gap is between "The" and a "Noun," it must be an Adjective. They should try swapping in a very simple word like "Red" or "Big." If "Big" fits the grammar, then the answer must be an adjective. Let the rules of the machine pick the part.
