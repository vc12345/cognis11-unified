Module 10: Constant Speed

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of Speed, Distance, and Time as the "Travel Trio." They are three sides of the same story. In the 11+, the examiner will give you two parts of the story and hide the third. The secret weapon is recognizing that Distance is the Boss—it sits at the top of everything. If you know how the trio connects, you can calculate how far, how fast, or how long in seconds.

The Toy Car "A remote-control car travels 20 metres in 4 seconds. What is its speed?"
The Fast Train "A train travels at 75mph. How far will it go in 3 hours?"
The Flight Path "A plane flies 1,200 miles at 400mph. How long does the journey take?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Magic Triangle. This is the logical map for your child. Tell them: "Cover the letter we are looking for with your thumb." What's left tells you the math. Distance (D) is on top because it is the total; Speed (S) and Time (T) are the partners at the bottom.

Phase 3: The Engine

Use the inputs to change the story. Help your child notice the logic shift: When we look for Distance, we multiply the bottom two. When we look for Speed or Time, we divide the top by the bottom.

TARGET: DISTANCE

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Clock Confuser": Bright children often forget that clocks and math don't speak the same language. If a car travels for 1 hour and 30 minutes, they will often plug 1.3 into the formula. This is a trap!

The "Divide by 60" Fix: Tell your child: "Minutes are just fractions of 60." 30 minutes is 0.5 of an hour (30/60). 15 minutes is 0.25. Always convert minutes into decimals before they touch the triangle, or the result will be a "Saboteur" answer.
