Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

Chronological alignment mirrors The Core Logic of Time. Every structural sentence template mandates an unwavering, unified timestamp boundary. The vast majority of operational errors manifest when a writer inadvertently lets their attention "slip" midway through a long clause. We only authorize a tactical shift in tense when the timeline itself explicitly jumps states—such as when an ongoing background action gets disrupted by a sudden obstacle (for instance, "I was sleeping when the alarm bells rang").

The Narrative Slip-Up: "She played tag and then eats a snack." (An unauthorized jump from a Past action straight to a Present detail).
The Universal Fact Clash: "The burner is hot and the cooling fan blew air." (Static scientific laws and environmental rules must always stay anchored inside the Present).
The Clause Interruption: "While we were winning the game, the downpour stops the match." (The intervening event must synchronize safely with its past background layer).

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Dialogue Quotation" Structural Illusion Trap: Children working quickly through errors frequently try to alter the inner tense of words trapped cleanly inside direct speech marks (for example, reading *The explorer stated, "I am exhausted"* and assuming 'am' must be changed to 'was'). This is an error! The speaker inside the quotation bubble always lives in their own private "now" moment, even if the surrounding narrative frame is set centuries in the past.

The Easy Fix: Execute the Anchor Verb Checklist. Train your student to follow this simple trick: "Isolate the narrative's opening verb." Locate the first active descriptive verb positioned completely outside of dialogue marks. This sets your absolute structural Anchor. Every background event running across that paragraph layout must align cleanly with that primary time stamp. If your anchor is verified as Past, your overall story must stay in the Past.
