Module 59: The Timekeeper (Tense Consistency)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Tense is simply The Logic of Time. Every sentence has a "time-stamp." If a story starts in the Past, it usually needs to stay there. Most mistakes happen when a writer accidentally "slips" into the Present mid-sentence. You only switch tenses when the timeline actually shifts—like when a long background action is interrupted by a quick event (e.g., "I was sleeping when the alarm rang").

The Common Slip "She played tag and then eats a snack."
The Glitch: Jumped from Past to Present.
The Logical Clash "They arrived late, so they miss the start."
The Glitch: The timeline is broken.
The Interruption Error "While we were winning, the rain stops the match."
The Glitch: The interruption doesn't match the background.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Quotation" Trap: Many children try to "fix" the tense inside speech marks. For example: The boy said, "I am happy." This is actually correct! Even if the story is in the past, the person talking is in their own "now."

The Fix: Use the Anchor Verb Test. Find the very first verb in the description (not the speech). This is your "Anchor." Every other action in the story must match that Anchor’s time-stamp. If the Anchor is past, the rest of the story must bow to the past.
