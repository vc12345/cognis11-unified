Module 46: Series Progression

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Detective's Film Strip." In the 11+, you are shown a row of boxes where a pattern is evolving step-by-step. The secret weapon is finding the Delta—the exact mathematical rule of change between Frame 1 and Frame 2. If a dot jumps two inches to the right in the first step, it must do the exact same thing in the final step. Instead of looking at the whole picture, your child should track just one tiny piece of the "film" at a time.

The Single Jump "A circle moves from the top-left to the bottom-right. Where will it land next?"
The Dual Rule "A shape rotates 90° clockwise AND flips its color from black to white in each step."
The 11+ Triple Threat "An object changes position, shading, and size all at once. Track them separately!"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Visual Overload" Trap: Most children try to "feel" the answer by staring at the whole frame until it makes sense. Their brain gets overwhelmed by all the moving parts and misses the one tiny detail that actually solves the puzzle.

The Insider Fix: The "Pinch" Method. Tell your child: "Divide and conquer." They should use their fingers to physically cover up everything in the box except for one element (like a tiny dot in the corner). They should solve the rule for that one dot first. Once they have it, they ignore that dot and move to the next shape. Three simple puzzles are easier to solve than one big one.
