Foundation Intro

The Anchor Face Strategy

To keep clear track of a solid block as it rolls through space, your child must select a visual Anchor Face. This represents the one specific target pattern you can see both before and after the object changes positions. By locking your attention onto this anchor, you can watch all neighboring symbols pivot around it like moons orbiting a planet.

"Look closely at our prominent Star face. It serves as our anchor landmark. Resting right next door to it is a Triangle. Click 'Spin the Block' below. Notice that our Star remains fully visible, but the Triangle has now rolled around onto the front face. The actual positioning relationship between the Star and Triangle never changed at all—only our point of view did!"

Foundation Progressing

The Hiding Parallel Face

In eleven-plus non-verbal spatial tests, you must constantly rely on the Law of Absolute Opposites. Every face on a cube possesses a fixed partner panel hiding on the exact opposite side. Because they are arranged completely back-to-back, it is physically impossible to view them at the same time.

"Examine this golden face labeled 'A'. Its parallel matching partner 'B' is positioned directly behind it at the rear of the block. Click 'Audit Opposite Logic'. Notice how 'B' stays locked out of sight? If an answer option on your worksheet shows panels A and B crowded next to each other, you instantly know it's a fake decoy. Opposite faces can never share a border!"
Rule: Parallel faces A and B can never touch.
Identify the partners to eliminate decoys.

Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

Approach block spinning problems as practicing rapid "3D Tracking Analysis." Solving these puzzles accurately relies on mapping out Adjacency Logic. Because you are only permitted to see three face panels simultaneously from any single angle, identify a primary landmark anchor and trace its border neighbors. If a Star sits directly above a Circle icon on the original reference card, it must remain positioned above that Circle regardless of how many horizontal spins are applied.

The Horizontal Pivot Track: "Calculate the look of a cube after a 90-degree sideways turn. Which neighboring panel assumes the front position?"
The Border Adjacency Audit: "Spot the 'Impossible Neighbor'—a distractor choice where a confirmed back-face partner is drawn touching the anchor."
The Directional Alignment Trap: "Verify whether an internal directional arrow points directly toward the Star face after a 180-degree tumble."

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Mirage Neighbor" Layout Trap: Test designers frequently craft distractor blocks that place all the correct symbols onto the visible panels, but arrange them in impossible configurations—such as showing two known Opposite Faces sharing a border line (like the Front symbol and Back symbol drawn side-by-side).

The Easy Fix: Enforce the Law of Invisible Opposites. Coach your child with this absolute strategy: "True opposite faces are always hidden from each other." If symbol A and symbol B sit on parallel opposite sides of the cube frame, they can never be caught in the same view. The very second you spot a choice displaying both together, cross it out immediately. It is an impossible layout.
