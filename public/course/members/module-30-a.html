Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Secret Service Code." Words are hidden behind a "shift"—where each letter moves a certain number of steps down the alphabet. The secret is Pattern Persistence. Once you find the "Key" (how many steps 'A' moved), you must apply that exact same jump to every letter to crack the code.

Single Step "If 'CAT' is 'DBU', what is the shift? Use it to code 'DOG'."
Decoder Ring "In code, 'FISH' becomes 'HKUJ'. Decode the mystery word 'BIRD'."
Reverse Riddle "The code is flipped (A=Z, B=Y). If the message is 'BCZ', find the word."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Concentric Cipher. The outer ring is "Normal"; the inner is "Secret". The gold line is the Sync Point. When you rotate the dial, every letter pair shifts together like locked gears. The distance between letters never changes.

Phase 3: The Engine

Rotate the inner dial and type your word. Watch the Trace Log translate each letter. If you can guess the shift for 'A', you can solve the whole word.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Alphabet Wall" Trap: Children often visualize the alphabet as a flat line. When a shift moves past 'Z', they freeze because they think the alphabet has "run out" of room.

The Fix: The "Alphabet Clock" Rule. "The alphabet is a ring, not a ruler." Just like a clock goes from 12 back to 1, the alphabet loops from Z back to A. **Z is just the bridge back to A.**
