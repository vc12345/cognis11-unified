Module 29: Alpha-Numeric Decoder

Foundation Intro

The Number Badges

Every letter of the alphabet has its own secret number badge. If we line the alphabet up, A gets badge 1, B gets badge 2, all the way to Z at badge 26. Decoding is just matching the letter to its badge!

"Look at the letters A, B, and C. They are wearing their standard badges. If I give you the code '3-1-2', can you find which letters those badges belong to? Tap the badges to see them light up!"

Foundation Progressing

The Alphabet Ruler

In harder codes, the badges "shift." If the shift is +2, then A doesn't get badge 1 anymore—it gets badge 3! The whole alphabet moves down the ruler together, like a train sliding along a track.

"Move the slider to shift the badges. Notice how the numbers stay in order, but the letters move to new partners. If A=4, then B must be 5. The pattern is always consistent!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+, examiners hide messages by swapping letters for numbers. Whether using a simple count ($A=1$), a reverse countdown ($A=26$), or a shift ($A=3$), the secret is Mapping Consistency. Once you find the "Key," apply it to every letter without breaking the pattern.

Direct "If $A=1, B=2$, what is the numerical code for 'FISH'?"
Reverse "If the alphabet is flipped ($A=26, Z=1$), what is 'CAT'?"
The Shift "A code uses a +3 offset ($A=4$). Decode '4-18-21-13' to find the word."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Cipher Dial. The gold line is your Master Key. When the dial rotates, the whole alphabet moves like locked gears—if you know one pair, you know them all. Notice the loop from Z back to A.

Phase 3: The Engine

Rotate the dial and type your message. Watch the Trace Log translate each letter using the active key. Try flipping the mapping direction!

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Alphabet Wall" Trap: When a shift moves past Z, children freeze. If $A=24$, they think $C$ is impossible because the alphabet "ended."

The Fix: The "Alphabet Clock" Rule. "The alphabet is a circle." Just like a clock goes from 12 back to 1, the alphabet goes from Z back to A. If you hit Z, keep counting from 1 at A.
