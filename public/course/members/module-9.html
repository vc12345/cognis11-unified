Module 9: Sequential Fractions

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "Remainder Trap." In these problems, the "Total" changes halfway through the story. Once the first slice of the pie is gone, the next fraction only cares about what is left on the plate. If your child can learn to ignore the original total and focus on the Remainder, they’ll dodge the most common mistake in the 11+.

The Sweet Jar "Sam has 20 sweets. He eats 4. He gives 1/2 of what is left to Sue. How many does he keep?"
The Library Book "A book has 120 pages. I read 1/3 on Monday, then 1/4 of the remainder on Tuesday. Pages left?"
The Travel Budget "A runner has £400. They spend 2/5 on a hotel and 1/3 of the remainder on travel. Final balance?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Watch the whole "pie" shrink. Point to the First Cut—this is where we find the first amount. Then, point to the Remainder. This grey bar becomes the "new 100%" for Stage 2. We aren't cutting the whole bar anymore; we are only cutting the piece that survived Stage 1.

Phase 3: The Engine

Move the "First Cut" numbers with your child. Notice how the **Stage 2 bar** immediately changes its size. Point out that the "Spent" amount in Stage 2 is a fraction of that new, smaller pile, not the original £600.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Original Total" Trap: Most children try to calculate the second fraction using the original starting total (e.g., finding 2/5 of £600 instead of 2/5 of the leftover £450).

The Fix: Circle the Keyword. Tell your child to look for the word "Remainder" or "Left" in the question. If they see it, they must draw a "Stop Sign" after Stage 1. They have to do a subtraction to find the new pile before they are allowed to touch the second fraction.
