Module 25: Combinatorics (Handshakes)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as the "Party Puzzle." In the 11+, you’ll often see problems about people shaking hands, teams playing matches, or friends exchanging gifts. The secret weapon is realizing that a handshake is a two-way street. If your child can visualize the "Grid of Everyone," they’ll see that most handshakes are just mirror images of each other. To find the unique interactions, we simply need to "clean" the grid of duplicates.

The High-Five "4 friends high-five each other once. How many high-fives happen total?"
The Tournament "8 teams play in a tournament. If every team plays every other team once, how many games are played?"
The Reverse Riddle "45 handshakes happened at a meeting. How many people were in the room?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Matrix of Possibility. Each row and column represents a person. Point to the diagonal line of dots—those are Self-Shakes (which don't happen!). When you click "Filter Grid," watch exactly half of the remaining squares vanish. Those are the Mirror Handshakes. We only keep one side of the diagonal because shaking hands with Person B is the same event as Person B shaking hands with you.

Phase 3: The Engine

Use the slider to add people to the room. Click the Filter button above to see the logic applied. Help your child notice the formula in the gold box: we multiply the people by $(n - 1)$ to remove themselves, then divide by 2 to remove the mirrors.

The Handshake Rule
n(n - 1) / 2

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Reflection" Trap: If there are 10 people, most children calculate $10 \times 9 = 90$ and stop there. They are counting every handshake twice—once for Person A and once for Person B!

The Insider Fix: The "Two-to-One" Rule. Remind your child: "It takes two people to make one handshake." Whenever they calculate pairings, they must always divide the total grid by 2 to get rid of the mirror images. If they don't divide, they aren't counting handshakes; they are counting hands! Always divide the group pairings by 2.
