Module 31: Vowel/Consonant Ciphers

Foundation Intro

The Sorting Doors

Imagine a sorting office with two doors. One door is **Blue** for Vowels (A, E, I, O, U) and the other is **Red** for all other letters (Consonants). Before we change a letter, we must first decide which door it belongs in.

"Click 'Generate Letter'. Watch as it moves toward the gates. If it's a Vowel, it enters the blue door. If it's a Consonant, it enters the red one. We can't solve the code until we know which door the letter uses!"
Ready to sort the alphabet.
Foundation Progressing

The Dual Conveyors

In advanced codes, the two groups have their own rules. The Vowel conveyor might move forward (+1) while the Consonant conveyor moves backward (-1). This means letters in the same word can jump in opposite directions!

"Look at the word BRAIN. The letters move on different tracks. B, R, and N are on the Red track. A and I are on the Blue track. Watch them move independently when you click 'Shift'."
Word: BRAIN. Rules: Vowels +1, Consonants -1.

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Sorting Office." The rule depends on what kind of letter you are holding. Vowels (A,E,I,O,U) and Consonants each have their own math. Act like a gatekeeper—categorize every letter through a mental "check" before shifting it.

Basic Gate "If Vowels shift +1 and Consonants stay same, find 'BAT'."
Split Shift "Vowels shift +2, Consonants shift -1. Find code for 'DOG'."
11+ Reverse "Reverse the rules to turn 'QMANFU' back into 'PLANET'."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Property Gatekeeper. Notice the color coding: Blue for Vowels, Red for Consonants. A single word can follow two different paths simultaneously. A vowel might jump forward while its neighbor jumps back.

Phase 3: The Engine

Type a word and set separate shifts. Watch the Trace Log sort the characters. Spot the moment the rule "flips" as the word moves between types.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Track" Trap: Children get into a rhythm. If they shift consonants by -1, they often shift vowels by -1 too by mistake.

The Fix: The "Label Before You Leap" Rule. "Don't do the math yet." Write a tiny 'V' or 'C' above every letter in the word first. Tagging stops the autopilot from ruining the code.
