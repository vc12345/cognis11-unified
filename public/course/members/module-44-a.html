Foundation Progressing

The T-Junction Secret

When two shapes overlap, their lines meet to form a letter **T**. The top of the **T** always belongs to the shape that is On Top. The "stem" of the **T** belongs to the shape hiding underneath.

"Look at where the circle meets the square. Click 'Find the Junctions'. See the red dots? Notice how the square's lines stop suddenly when they hit the circle. The square is the 'loser'—it is at the back!"
The shape that "breaks" its lines is at the back.

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Pancake Rule." Examiners overlap shapes to test if you can identify the "last dropped" object. The secret is Perimeter Continuity. If a shape has an unbroken border, it is on top. If the border is interrupted, it is underneath. Peel back the layers mentally by scanning for line breaks.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Same Color" Trap: If two shapes share a color, the brain merges them. You miss the fact that one is hiding behind the other.

The Fix: The "T-Junction" Hunt. "Look for the Ts." Ignore the color; look at where lines meet. If Shape A's line stops at Shape B's edge (a 'T'), Shape A is at the back. **Top shapes never stop their lines.**
