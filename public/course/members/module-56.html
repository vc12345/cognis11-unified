Module 56: Cloze (Contextual)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of contextual cloze as "Semantic Radar." In the 11+, you are hunting for Magnet Words—specific clues that physically pull a certain vocabulary match toward the gap. Success isn't about guessing what fits; it's about reading past the hole to find the resolution. If a sentence mentions an "empty water bottle" and a "desert," the missing word is mathematically forced to be parched, not just unhappy.

The Content Cat "The cat ______ as it curled by the warm fire."
Magnet Words: Content / Warm Fire. Fit: Purred.
The Hungry Hiker "He was so ______ that he ate his dinner in seconds."
Magnet Words: Ate / Seconds. Fit: Famished.
The 11+ Politician "The leader’s ______ speech failed to convince the crowd."
Magnet Words: Failed / Skeptical. Fit: Vague.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Sounds Right" Trap: Most children choose a word because it fits a common phrase in their head, even if it contradicts the clues in the sentence. For example, they might pick "The hiker felt happy" because it sounds nice, completely ignoring the fact that he is lost and out of water.

The Insider Fix: The "Evidence Check." Tell your child: "No word is an island." Before they commit, they must point to at least two Magnet Words in the sentence that support their choice. If they pick 'Parched,' their magnets are 'Empty bottle' and 'Arid.' If they can only find one magnet, the word is a distractor. Evidence beats 'feeling' every time.
