Module 40: Odd One Out

Foundation Intro

The Impostor Search

An "Odd One Out" puzzle is like looking at a group of friends where one person is wearing a different uniform. To find them, you don't look for what is "special"—you look for the Rule that everyone else is following.

"Look at these shapes. Three are Blue Squares. One is a Gold Circle. The rule is 'Blue Square'. Click the Gold Circle to unmask the impostor! Notice that you didn't have to study every square to know that the circle didn't belong."
Find the shape that breaks the pattern.

The Property Audit

In the 11+, words belong to "Clubs." If 3 words belong to the Fruit Club and 1 belongs to the Vegetable Club, the vegetable is the odd one out. You only need to check the first 3 items to find the club's name!

"Look at these items: **Apple**, **Pear**, **Peach**, **Onion**. Click 'Audit Club' to assign the Property Tags. Once we see that 3 items share the 'Fruit' tag, the 'Vegetable' tag stands out like a sore thumb. That's our answer!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as the "Group Gatekeeper." You are looking for a common bond that connects four of the words. The secret weapon isn't reading—it's speed-sorting. Find a shared property between the first few words to lock the rule. The first word that snaps that pattern is your target.

Kitchen "Apple, Banana, Carrot, Grape, Pear." (Veg among the fruits).
Minerals "Silver, Gold, Copper, Iron, Wood." (Non-metal outlier).
Nuance "Brave, Bold, Fearless, Reckless, Courageous." (Positive traits vs negative).

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Logic Sieve. We establish a "Majority Rule" one word at a time. The moment we hit the 2 vs 1 threshold, the answer is locked. The remaining "?" masks are just distractions that cost you time.

Phase 3: The Engine

Move the outlier to different positions. Notice that no matter where it hides, we usually stop thinking by the third or fourth box. This Stop-Point Logic is the key to finishing on time.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Linear Scan" Trap: Most children read all 5 words before they even start categorizing. This wastes 10 seconds per question on dead information.

The Fix: The "2 + 1" Rule. "The answer is revealed at word three." If Word 1 and 2 match, the rule is set. If Word 3 is different, that's the answer—stop! The moment you have a pair and an oddity, the logic is closed.
