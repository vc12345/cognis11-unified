Foundation Intro

The Time Stamp

Every story has a "Time Stamp." If the first action happens in the Past, the whole sentence needs to stay in the Past. Tense consistency is just making sure all your verbs are living in the same era.

The Timeline Glitch

In the 11+, examiners love to hide one "Present" verb inside a "Past" sentence. To find it, you must audit the timeline. If a verb is standing in the wrong spot, it is a **Glitch**.

"Read this: 'She **played** tag and then **eats** a snack.' One verb is in the Past, but the other is in the Present. Click 'Audit Timeline' to see the Glitch. We must move 'eats' back to the Past to fix the sentence!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Tense is The Logic of Time. Every sentence requires a consistent timestamp. Most errors occur when a writer "slips" mid-sentence. We only shift tenses when the timeline intentionally moves—such as when a background action is interrupted (e.g., "I was sleeping when the alarm rang").

The Story Slip "She played tag and then eats a snack." (Jumped Past to Present).
Fact Clash "The burner is hot and the fan blew air." (Facts remain in Present).
Interruption "While we were winning, the rain stops the match." (Interruption must match background).

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Interruption Timeline. Background actions (Long) set the scene, while Trigger actions (Short) break in. Both belong to the past, but they occupy different layers of the timeline. Audit the hierarchy to ensure they are synchronized.

Background: "I was eating"
Trigger: "Phone Rang"
Result: "I stopped"

Phase 3: The Engine

Audit the diagnostic passages. Click the word that creates a "Timeline Glitch" (an inconsistent tense) and then repair the logic to synchronize the sequence.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Quotation" Trap: Children often "fix" tense inside speech marks. (e.g. *The boy said, "I am happy"*). This is correct! The speaker is in their own "now" even if the story is Past.

The Fix: The "Anchor Verb Test." "Find the first verb." Identify the first descriptive verb (outside speech). This is the Anchor. Every other action in the narrative must match its time-stamp. **Anchor is Past = Story is Past.**
