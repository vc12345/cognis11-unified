Module 17: Reverse Operations

Foundation Intro

The Magic Undo Machine

Every action in math has a "twin" that does the exact opposite. If I give you 5 sweets (+5), the only way to get back to where you started is to give 5 sweets back (-5). We call this Inversing. It’s like hitting the "Rewind" button on a video.

"Look at our Magic Machine. It's currently set to 'Add 5'. When we push a number through, it grows. To find our starting number, we have to flip the switch to 'Take 5' and watch the number retrace its steps!"

The Inverse Key

Retracing a journey means flipping the signs. If the story says "Multiply by 2," the detective says "Divide by 2." If the story says "Subtract 10," the detective says "Add 10." The Inverse Key unlocks the path backwards to the start.

"We know the result is 20. The last step was 'Multiply by 2'. To retrace, we use the Inverse Key: 'Divide by 2'. Watch how we use the opposite sign to unlock each step until we reach the starting number."

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "Detective Method." In "I think of a number" problems, you are given the crime scene (the final result). To find the culprit, your child must retrace every step in total reverse. Every instruction must be flipped to its opposite (Inverse) to unlock the original number.

Single Step "I add 8 and get 20. What was my starting number?"
Double Jump "I multiply by 4, then subtract 6. The result is 30. Find the number."
The Challenge "I square a number, add 5, then divide by 3 to get 10. Starting point?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Retrace the Mirror Bridge. To go forward, follow the blue path. To find the start, use the gold Reverse Logic. We flip the signs (Add → Sub; Mul → Div) and retrace from right to left.

Phase 3: The Engine

Build a multi-step problem. Watch the Trace Log unravel the logic. Remember: the very last step performed is always the first one we undo.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "First-Come" Trap: Children often try to reverse the first instruction they read. This is like taking off socks before shoes—it's impossible!

The Fix: The "Shoes and Socks" Rule. The last thing you did is the first thing you undo. Always start at the finish line and walk backwards.
