Module 22: 3-Circle Venn Logic

Foundation Intro

The Triple Topping

A 3-circle Venn is just a more complex sorting puzzle. Some people want Sprinkles, some want Flakes, and some want Sauce. But look at the center—that's for the person who wants ALL THREE. They are one person, but they live in three lists at once!

"We have a 'Mega Sundae' with Sprinkles, a Flake, and Sauce. Where does it go? It satisfies all three rules. Click 'Place Sundae' to see it find the only spot on the map where all three circles overlap."
Find the home for the triple-topping treat.
Foundation Progressing

The Onion Peeler

To solve a 3-circle puzzle, you must Peel the Onion. You start at the very center (the Core) and work your way out. If 5 people are in the center, you must subtract those 5 from every other overlapping section before you can find the final total.

"Look at the 'A & B' overlap. It says 10. But we know 4 of those people are in the 'All 3' core. Click 'Peel Layer' to subtract the core. Now we see there are only 6 people who like ONLY A & B. We always start from the middle and move out!"
The overlap looks like 10... but is it "Clean"?

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of the 3-circle Venn as the "Onion Challenge." In the 11+, you are usually given the "skin" (the big group totals) and asked to find a specific layer inside. The secret is Inside-Out Deduction. Ignore the big numbers at first and find the Core (All 3). Once you anchor that center point, you peel back the overlaps one by one.

The Ice Cream "5 children like all 3 flavors. 12 like Vanilla and Choc. How many like Vanilla/Choc but NOT Strawberry?"
The Sports "15 Football, 15 Rugby, 15 Cricket. 4 play all three. Each pair-overlap is 7. Total unique students?"
The Logic "Find the population where every intersection is known. Use the logic of 'Cleaning' the numbers from the center."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Shared Core. Click "Commence Merger" to see the groups collide. The red number in the center is the "Boss"—it has been subtracted from every other section to ensure no one is cloned. The center is the only number that starts out honest.

Phase 3: The Engine

Set your group sizes. Watch the Trace Log to see the sequence of subtractions. Notice how the **Center Core** is used first for every calculation.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Triple-Counting" Trap: If you add the group totals (25+25+25), the people in the Core are counted three times! You've accidentally invented ghost students.

The Fix: The "Onion Peel" Rule. "Clean from the middle out." Subtract the Core from the pair-overlaps first. Then subtract those and the core from the main totals. **The center is your anchor.**
