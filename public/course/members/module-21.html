Module 21: Venn Diagrams (2 Circles)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of a Venn Diagram as "The Land of Both." In the 11+, examiners love to set traps by giving you two separate group totals that overlap. The secret weapon is the Intersection ($A \cap B$)—the sweet spot where things satisfy both rules at once. If your child can visualize those shared items, they'll realize that simply adding the two groups together creates a "double count" that breaks the logic. To find the truth, we must always subtract the overlap from the totals to see who is "Only" in a group.

The Football/Rugby Club "In a class of 12 footballers, 3 also play rugby. How many play football ONLY?"
The Group Merge "Group A has 20 people, Group B has 15. If 5 people are in both, how many people are there in total?"
The Minimum Secret "Out of 40 students, 25 like Art and 20 like Music. What is the smallest number of students that must like both?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Blind Addition" Trap: Most children see "15 Footballers" and "10 Swimmers" and immediately say "25 people total." They are assuming that nobody plays both! If 5 people actually do both, your child has just accidentally invented 5 extra people.

The Insider Fix: The "Once Only" Rule. Remind your child: "Each person has one seat." To find the real total, they must subtract the intersection from both sides first. The secret formula is: $Total = (A - Intersection) + (B - Intersection) + Intersection$. Clean the groups first, then add them up.
