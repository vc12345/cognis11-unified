Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

Think of this question structure as resolving an "Expendable Tile Migration" logic puzzle. A single character jumps out of the leading word grid and drops straight into the trailing row. The vital secret here is identifying that the moving piece must leave a fully functioning, legitimate word behind in its original location. This represents a symmetrical double-verification check: the movement must prove entirely vocabulary-correct on both sides of the puzzle card.

The Base Swap Row: "Migrate one isolated character from the word block CHART over into EAT."

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "One-Sided Target" Illusion Trap: Children under pressure often spot a letter that constructs a brilliant new option inside the second box (like moving the H out of CHART to build HEAT) and log their answer right away. They completely forget to check if the leftover base is a real word (confirming that CART is a valid standalone term!).

The Easy Fix: Apply the Dual Dictionary Balance Check. Keep your child mindful of this concept: "The traveling letter is a bridge that must hold weight on both ends simultaneously." Train them to perform two quick, distinct checks every time: 1. Is the source word still alive and real? 2. Is the destination word correctly born? Both conditions must pass to win the mark.
