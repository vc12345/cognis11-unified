Foundation Intro

The Spinner Rule

Imagine a slice of pizza. Whether the crust is pointing Up, Down, Left, or Right, it is still the same slice of pizza. It hasn't changed its identity—it's just having a spin!

"Look at our pizza slice. It has a single pepperoni on the right side. Click 'Spin Pizza'. Notice that as the slice turns, the pepperoni stays on the same side of the crust. If it suddenly jumped to the other side, it would be a different slice!"
Identity check: Pepperoni is on the right.

To keep track of a shape during a turn, you need an **Anchor**. Find a unique corner (like the "elbow" of an L-shape) and watch where it goes. As long as the features stay in the same order, the shape is valid.

"Look at this 'L' shape. Notice the gold 'Anchor' on the long arm. Click 'Rotate 90°'. Even though the shape is lying down now, the anchor is still on that same long arm. If the anchor moved to the short arm, the shape would be an impostor!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "Mental Acrobatics." A shape is technically identical no matter which way it faces—as long as it’s just turned. The secret weapon is picking a unique feature (a Tracker) and checking its relative position. If the Tracker switches sides, it’s a Reflection, not a rotation.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Mirror" Trap: The examiner shows a shape that has been Reflected (flipped) but turned to the correct angle. It looks right at first glance!

The Fix: The "Clockwise Rule." "Trace the features in a circle." In a rotation, the order of details (Pole → Arm → Dot) is always clockwise. In a reflection, the order flips to anti-clockwise. **If the order breaks, it's a fake.**
