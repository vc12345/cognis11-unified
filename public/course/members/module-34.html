Module 34: Compound Word Bridge

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as the "Word Hinge." In the 11+, you are looking for one single word that can glue two separate words together. This mystery word must act as the end of the first word and the start of the second. The secret weapon is dual-scanning: your child must find a word that is strong enough to hold up both sides of the bridge at the same time.

The Weather Game "Find the word that fits: RAIN [ ____ ] BALL. It must make a new word with both."
The Four-Letter Link "Find the bridge: FOOT [ ____ ] ROOM. Both must be real compound nouns."
The Sea Captain "Identify the bridge for SEA [ ____ ] SIDE. The hinge must click into place perfectly."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Half-Match" Trap: Most children find a word that fits perfectly with the first block (like RAIN-COAT) and get so excited they forget to check the second block. They write down "COAT" even though "COAT-BALL" isn't a word!

The Insider Fix: The "Tug-of-War" Rule. Tell your child: "The bridge word is being pulled from both sides." Once they have a candidate, they must mentally pull it over to the second word. If it doesn't "click" instantly to form a common word they recognize, it's a fake. It must work on both sides to be true.
