Module 6: Percentage Multipliers

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+, doing percentage problems in two steps (finding 10%, then doubling it, then adding it) is a "Speed Trap." We use the One-Step Shortcut. By treating the original price as 1.00, we simply multiply once to get straight to the final answer. This turns a messy word problem into a 5-second calculation.

The Toy Sale "A £20 teddy is 10% off. How much is the new price?"
The Marble Bonus "40 marbles get a 20% 'extra free' bonus. What is the new total?"
The Price Hike "A £40 item increases by 15%. What is the new cost?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Think of the Starting Block. The blue bar is always your 100% (the number 1.00). An increase stretches that bar forward, while a decrease cuts it back. Notice how the "Shortcut Number" in the gold bubble tells you exactly how much bar you have left.

Phase 3: The Engine

Toggle between increase and decrease with your child. Watch the Shortcut Number in the gold bubble. Help them notice that a 20% increase is just multiplying by 1.2, while a 20% decrease is just multiplying by 0.8.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Simple Addition" Trap: If a price is £50 and goes up 10%, many kids just add the number 10 (making it £60). They are treating the percentage like a £10 note instead of a part of the original price.

The Fix: The "Shortcut Multiplier." Remind your child: "Percentages are for multiplying, not adding." Turn the 10% into its shortcut (1.1) and multiply: 50 × 1.1 = £55. This keeps the "extra" proportionate to the starting value.
