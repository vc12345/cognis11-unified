Module 4: Inverse Proportion

Foundation Intro

The Tidy-Up Test

Usually, in math, "more" of one thing means "more" of another thing. But in Inverse Proportion, more helpers means less time. Think of it as tidying Lego: if you work alone, it takes a long time. If three friends help, the job is finished in a flash!

"Look at the pile of Lego. If we have 1 helper putting away the pile, the clock shows 12 minutes. Add more friends to see how much faster it goes!"

Foundation Progressing

The See-Saw Balance

In inverse proportion, there is a Magic Number that never changes. It’s like a See-Saw. If you put more "Workers" on one side, the "Time" on the other side must go down to keep the balance.

"If we have 1 worker taking 12 hours, our Magic Number is 12. If we increase to 4 workers, the see-saw tips until the time drops to 3 to keep the balance!"
Workers × Time = 12 (Total Work)

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "More is More" Trap: Children often think doubling workers doubles time. **Wrong!**

The Fix: The "Lego Test." More friends = Less time. **Divide** the Magic Number by the new helpers.
