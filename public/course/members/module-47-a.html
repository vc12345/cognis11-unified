Module 47: Valid Cube Nets

Foundation Intro

The Sticky Tape Test

Imagine you have a flattened cardboard box. To make it a cube, you have to fold the edges until they touch. If two edges can be taped together without overlapping or leaving a gap, the net is Valid.

"Look at these two faces on the net. They look far apart, but when the box folds, they become neighbors! Click 'Test Edges' to see the gold tape join them together. This is how 2D shapes become 3D objects."
Edges are separate in 2D. Ready to tape!
Foundation Progressing

The Leapfrog Bridge

The fastest way to solve cube nets is to find Opposite Pairs. In any straight line of three or more squares, the faces separated by exactly one jump will be opposites. They can never touch in 3D!

"Look at the long row of four squares. If we start at Face A, we 'leapfrog' over Face B to find Face C. A and C are opposites. Click 'Bridge Pairs' to see every partner on this net."
Straight lines hide the best clues.

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A cube net is a "Flattened Cardboard Box." Success depends on identifying Opposite Pairs. Since opposites can never touch and can never be seen together, finding them is the fastest way to eliminate decoy answers. Use the Leapfrog Rule: in a line of three squares, the outer two are partners.

Standard Cross Identify the "Base" if "Top" is selected in a 1-4-1 net.
The T-Shape Find all three opposite pairs in a T-style layout.
Advanced "Z" Solve a "Z-Net" where squares are staggered.

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Pairing Map. Click any numbered face. The engine highlights its partner in Red. They jump over exactly one face. If two numbers are highlighted as opposites, they can never share an edge on the finished cube.

Phase 3: The Engine

Swap layouts to build mental muscle. Challenge: "If I pick Face 2, where is its parallel partner?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Touching Corner" Trap: Children assume squares sharing a corner on flat paper must touch on the cube. **Illusion!** Many distant faces meet at the same edge in 3D.

The Fix: The "Opposite Shield." "Look for what CAN'T touch." Find the three opposite pairs. If an answer shows two opposites sharing a line, cross it out immediately. **No second-guessing.**
