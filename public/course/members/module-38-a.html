Module 38: Synonym Precision

Foundation Intro

The Volume Knob

Synonyms aren't always equal. Some words are "whispers" (weak) and some are "shouts" (intense). Choosing the right word is like turning up a volume dial until it matches the drama of the story.

"We are describing a slight wind. A 'Gentle' breeze is a low volume. But a 'Violent' hurricane is high volume! Click the buttons to turn the dial. Notice how the word grows and changes color as it gets louder."
Gentle
The word 'Gentle' is low intensity.

The Emotion Meter

To win at the 11+, you must learn to rank words from 1 to 100. If a character is crying, the word "sad" (level 20) might be too weak. You might need "distraught" (level 90) to match the speed of the drama.

"Look at these synonyms for 'Happy'. **Glad**, **Joyful**, and **Ecstatic**. Move the slider to see where they sit on the meter. See how 'Ecstatic' pushes the needle all the way into the red zone?"
Glad
Glad is a mild intensity emotion.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Volume Mismatch" Trap: Choosing a synonym that is accurate but "underpowered" (e.g., describing an explosion as "noisy" instead of "cataclysmic").

The Fix: The "Drama Test." "Check the neighbors." If the sentence uses high-drama words like *shattered* or *instantly*, choose the synonym from the extreme red end of the spectrum.
