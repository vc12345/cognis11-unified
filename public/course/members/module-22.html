Module 22: 3-Circle Venn Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of the 3-circle Venn as the "Onion Challenge." In the 11+, you are usually given the "skin" (the big group totals) and asked to find a specific "layer" inside. The secret is Inside-Out Deduction. To solve these, you must ignore the big numbers at first and find the Core (the items that fit all three rules). If your child can anchor that center point, they can peel back the layers one by one to find the truth.

The Ice Cream Shop "5 children like all 3 flavors. 12 like Vanilla and Choc. How many like Vanilla and Choc but NOT Strawberry?"
The Sports Day "15 play Football, 15 Rugby, 15 Cricket. 4 play all three. Each pair-overlap is 7. How many unique students are there?"
The Logic Master "Find the population of a 3-set universe where every intersection is known. Use the logic of 'Cleaning' the numbers."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Triple-Counting" Trap: If your child adds the group totals together (e.g., $25 + 25 + 25 = 75$), they have just accidentally cloned the people in the middle. The people in the "Core" are currently living in all three circles at once—they are being counted three times!

The Fix: The "Onion Peel" Rule. Tell your child: "Clean from the middle out." They must subtract the Core from the pair-overlaps first. Then, they must subtract those new results and the core from the main circles. Only when every section is "cleaned" can they safely add them up. The center is the only number that starts out honest.
