The Anchor Face

To keep track of a cube as it spins, you need to pick an **Anchor**. This is the one face you can see before AND after the turn. If you watch the anchor, you can see its neighbors move like planets orbiting a sun.

"Look at the Star face. It's our anchor. On its right is a Triangle. Click 'Spin Right'. Notice that the Star is still there, but the Triangle has moved to the front. The relationship between the Star and Triangle never changed—only our view of them did!"

The Invisible Partner

In the 11+, you must remember the **Law of Opposites**. Every face on a cube has a partner hiding on the exact opposite side. Since they are back-to-back, you can never see them at the same time.

"Look at the Gold face marked 'A'. Its partner 'B' is directly behind it. Click 'Reveal Opposites'. Notice how 'B' stays hidden? If a multiple-choice answer shows A and B together, it's an impostor. Opposites are too shy to be seen together!"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Mirage Neighbor" Trap: Distractors place correct symbols in impossible spots, showing two **Opposite** faces touching (e.g. Front and Back).

The Fix: The "Law of Opposites." "Opposites are shy." If Symbol A and B are on opposite sides, they can **never** be seen simultaneously. If an answer shows both, it's a lie. Cross it out immediately.
