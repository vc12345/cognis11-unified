Module 54: Tone & Purpose

Foundation Intro

The Mood Mask

Every story has a **Purpose** (the job it’s doing) and a **Tone** (how it feels). Imagine you are telling a friend that it is raining. You can say it as a fact, a joke, or a complaint. The rain is the same, but your "Mask" changes!

"We are telling people it is raining. That's our Purpose: To Inform. But look at our face. Click 'Change Tone' to see the difference between a 'Sad' rainy day and a 'Grumpy' rainy day. The news is the same, but the feeling is different!"

The Word Palette

In the 11+, you can "paint" a sentence to change its tone. **Verbs** usually tell you the Purpose (what is happening), while **Adjectives** tell you the Tone (how the author feels about it).

"Look at this sentence: 'The **massive** giant **stomped**.' Click 'Analyze' to see the paint colors. 'Stomped' is our Purpose Verb—it tells us he is moving. 'Massive' is our Tone Adjective—it makes him sound scary and powerful."

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "The Author's Intent." Tone is the emotional temperature—how the author feels. Purpose is the mechanical job the text is doing—why it was written. By isolating Verbs and Adjectives, we reveal if the writer is trying to help, trick, or ridicule.

Rule-Maker "Clean your room!" Tone: Stern. Purpose: Command.
Story-Teller "Golden sun dipped below velvet clouds." Tone: Serene. Purpose: Describe.
11+ Critic "A 'miracle' as useful as a chocolate teapot." Tone: Sarcastic. Purpose: Ridicule.

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Semantic Ratio. Purpose is usually hidden in the Verbs (Action), while Tone is painted with Adjectives (Feeling). Swapping the adjectives while keeping the verbs preserves the action but shifts the vibe completely.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Topic" Trap: Children mistake the *topic* for the *tone*. If the story is about rain, they assume the tone is "sad." They are reacting to the weather, not the words.

The Fix: The "Adjective Audit." "Ignore the story, look at the paint." List only the descriptive words. If a sad event uses "standard" and "noted," the tone is **Clinical**, not sad. **Tone lives in choice, not plot.**
