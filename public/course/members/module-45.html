Module 45: Attribute Matrices

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of a matrix as "The Logic Intersection." In the 11+, your child will see a 3x3 grid where shapes evolve like a puzzle. The secret weapon is realizing that every cell is governed by two separate laws: the Row Rule (what changes left-to-right) and the Column Rule (what changes top-to-bottom). The missing piece is simply the "fusion" of these two rules. If Row 3 says "Circles" and Column 3 says "Green," then the answer must be a green circle.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Track" Trap: Most children find a single pattern (e.g., "all the shapes in the bottom row are circles") and immediately pick the first circle they see in the options. This is a trap. The examiner will put three different circles in the options, each with a different color or tilt.

The Insider Fix: The "Cross-Hair" Method. Tell your child: "Split the evidence." Have them isolate Attribute 1 (Shape) by looking only at the rows. Then, isolate Attribute 2 (Color or Rotation) by looking only at the columns. They should write these two facts down separately. The correct answer must be the perfect "sum" of both facts. Never guess until both patterns are found.
