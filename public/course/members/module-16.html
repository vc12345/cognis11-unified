Module 16: The "More Than" Balance Rule

Foundation Intro

The Secret Reward

If you have a pile of 10 shiny stickers to hand out, but you want Sarah to finish with 2 more than Tom, you cannot simply hand out 5 to each of them. If you make a direct split, neither child has a larger share! The trick is to give Sarah her extra "Reward" first, set it aside, and then share out the remainder completely evenly.

"Imagine we are sharing 10 reward stickers. Sarah must finish with 2 more than Tom. First, click the 'Give Reward' button—we slide Sarah's 2 extra stickers onto her plate immediately. Now, look at the 8 stickers left in our main pile. We split those down the middle: 4 for Sarah and 4 for Tom. Sarah finishes with 6 and Tom has 4. Notice how the gap matches perfectly!"

Foundation Progressing

The Tower Haircut

When you look at two unequal block towers and you are given their Combined Height, sorting out the separate totals looks puzzling at first glance. But if you give the taller tower a visual "Haircut" by snipping off that extra overhang, both towers instantly transform into identical twins. Once they match, you can easily divide the remaining total by 2!

"These two towers hold 20 building blocks combined. Tower A is exactly 4 blocks taller than its partner. If we use our 'Snip' tool to slice those 4 extra blocks off, the 16 blocks left behind must be shared perfectly: 8 for each side. Now we know Tower B holds 8, and Tower A holds those same 8 plus the 4 blocks we safely cut off earlier!"

Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

This is famously known in competitive exams as the "Fake Half-and-Half" challenge. It pops up whenever a word problem gives you a Combined Total paired with a specific Gap Value (the difference). The default instinct is to divide the core total by 2 right away—avoid this trap! If you split the total first, your results will never balance. The solution is to isolate the extra reward amount (the gap) entirely before your hand touches the division key.

The Sticker Share: "Two close classmates have 12 collectible stickers in total. Sarah has 4 more stickers than Tom. How many stickers belong to Tom?"
The Computer Tablet: "A multimedia tablet and its protective case cost £250 combined. The tablet is worth £200 more than the case. Find the true cost of the case."
The Combined Ages: "A father and his son have a combined age of 50 years. The father is exactly 26 years older than his son. How old is the son?"

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Impulse Split" Trap: If an exam question states that a £250 total bundle includes a £200 gap difference, children often confidently declare: "The case is £50, and the tablet is £200." This is a classic trap! A price tag of £200 is only £150 larger than £50. Your structural gap failed to hold up!

The Easy Fix: The "Snip and Share" Rule. Train your child to subtract the gap first: £250 minus £200 leaves £50. Now share out that clean remainder evenly: £50 divided by 2 gives £25. The smaller case is worth exactly £25. The larger tablet is worth £25 plus our £200 gap, which equals £225. Always Snip your gaps before you Split your bars.
