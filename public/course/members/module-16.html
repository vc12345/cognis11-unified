Module 16: The "More Than" Trap

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "Fake 50/50" problem. It occurs when you are given a Combined Total and a Gap (the difference) between two people. Your child's instinct will be to divide the total by 2 immediately—but that is a trap set by the examiner. If you divide first, the math will never balance.

The Sticker Swap "Two friends have 12 stickers. Sarah has 4 more than Tom. How many does Tom have?"
The Tech Bundle "A tablet and a case cost £250. The tablet is £200 more than the case. What is the cost of the case?"
The Family Tree "A father and son are 50 years old combined. The father is 26 years older than the son. How old is the son?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

To beat this trap, we must "Snip the Gap." Imagine Person A and Person B each have a stack of money. Person A has an extra pile (the gap). If we physically snip that extra pile off and put it aside, the two stacks left on the table are exactly equal. Point to the red "Snip" line below—everything to the left of that line is a Fair Share.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Impulse Split" Trap: If the tablet and case cost £250, and the tablet is £200 more, most children will say the case is £50 ($250 \div 5$) and the tablet is £200. Stop! If the case is £50 and the tablet is £200, the tablet is only £150 more! The gap is wrong.

The Fix: The "Snip and Share" Rule. Remind your child: "The Gap is a thief." You must take the thief's portion out of the total first. £250 - £200 = £50. Now share the remaining £50 between the two items (£25 each). The case is £25, and the tablet is £25 + £200 = £225. Always subtract the gap first.
