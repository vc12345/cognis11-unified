Module 17: Reverse Operations

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the "Detective Method." In "I think of a number" problems, you aren't given the starting point—you are given the crime scene (the final result). To find the culprit, your child must retrace every step in total reverse. The world turns upside down: every instruction must be flipped to its opposite (Inverse) to unlock the original number.

The Single Step "I think of a number. I add 8 and get 20. What was my starting number?"
The Double Jump "I multiply a number by 4, then subtract 6. The result is 30. Find the number."
The 11+ Challenge "I square a number, add 5, then divide by 3 to get 10. What did I start with?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Think of this as The Mirror Bridge. To go forward, we follow the blue path. To find the start, we use the gold Reverse Logic. We flip the signs (Addition becomes Subtraction; Multiplication becomes Division) and retrace the path from right to left.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "First-Come" Trap: Children often try to reverse the very first instruction written in the sentence because it’s the first one they see. This is like trying to take off your socks before your shoes—it’s physically impossible!

The Fix: The "Shoes and Socks" Rule. Remind your child: "The last thing you did is the first thing you undo." Always retrace from the final result. If the result was the result of Step 2, you must undo Step 2 before you even look at Step 1. Start from the finish line.
