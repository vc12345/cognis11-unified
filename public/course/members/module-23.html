Module 23: The Average Family

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of averages as finding the "Center of Gravity" for a group of numbers. In the 11+, there are three siblings in the family: the Mean (the fair sharer), the Median (the middle person in a line-up), and the Mode (the most popular choice). Your child needs to know exactly which tool to grab based on the "story" the examiner is telling.

The Fair Share "Find the mean of 3, 5, and 7. If you shared these points equally, what would everyone get?"
The Line Up "Find the median of: 15, 4, 9, 15, 2. Who stands in the exact middle of the queue?"
The Missing Link "The mean age of four children is 12. Three children are 10, 11, and 14. How old is the fourth?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Jumbled Median" Trap: Most children look at a list like "8, 2, 5" and pick 2 as the Median because it’s in the middle of the sentence. This is a mistake. The Median doesn't care about the sentence; it only cares about the size.

The Fix: The "Parade" Rule. Tell your child: "Numbers must stand in height order." You cannot find a Median until the numbers are sorted from Smallest to Largest. If there is an even number of items, find the tiny "Mean" between the two people in the very center.
