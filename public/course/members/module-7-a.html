Module 7: Reverse Percentages

Foundation Intro

The Undo Button

Every percentage change is like a machine. If a "Double Machine" (200%) turns a small toy into a big one, the only way to fix it is to run the machine backwards. Reverse percentages are just using the "Undo" button to find out what we started with.

"Look at this gold shape. It’s been changed by the machine. We know it’s now 200% of its original size. If we want to find the original blue shape, we have to 'Undo' the growth. Watch what happens when we reverse the power!"

The Unitary Bridge

If we don't know the 100% (the start), we look for a smaller "stepping stone." If 20% of a price is £10, we can easily find 10% or 1%, and then walk the bridge all the way back to the full 100%. We always find "The One" first.

"We know that 80% of the price is £40. We can't jump to 100% in one go! Let's find 10% first by dividing by 8. Once we have that small piece, we can multiply it by 10 to cross the bridge to the full price."

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Reverse Percentages are about Time Travel. Usually, we start at the beginning and work forward. Here, we are at the "Destination" and need to Rewind. Since multiplication moved us forward, we must use Division to drive the car back to the start.

The Sale "A toy costs £8 after a 20% discount. What was the original price?"
The Hike "A rare card is worth £120 after a 50% increase. What was it worth before?"
The Goal "90% of a journey is 180km. How long is the whole trip?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Forward Percent" Trap: If a shirt is £90 after a 10% discount, children often find 10% of £90 (£9) and add it back to get £99. Stop! This is a trap. That 10% was stolen from a bigger number, not the £90.

The Fix: The "Division Stretch." Never calculate a percentage of the sale price. Find the Multiplier first (like 0.90) and DIVIDE. Division is the only tool that "stretches" the price back to its original size.
