Module 13: Train & Tunnel

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+, a train isn't just a point moving on a line—it’s a long object that takes time to fully "arrive" and fully "leave." Most children forget that for a train to pass through a tunnel, the nose has to enter, but the tail has to clear the exit. This means the total distance traveled is actually the length of the tunnel plus the length of the train itself.

The Signal Post "A 50m train passes a signal post. How far does the front travel before the back clears the post?"
The Mountain Tunnel "A 100m train goes through a 300m tunnel. What is the total distance covered to be completely out?"
The Speed Calculation "A 200m train passes a 400m platform in 30 seconds. What is the train's speed in m/s?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Front-Door" Trap: Most children only use the tunnel length (e.g., 400m) for their calculation. If they do this, their speed or time will be wrong because, at 400m, the train's nose has exited, but its body is still stuck inside the tunnel!

The Fix: The "String and Tube" Rule. Tell your child: "Imagine pulling a 2m piece of string through a 4m toilet roll tube. To get the whole string out, you have to pull it 6m in total." The secret is simple: Always add the Train + the Tunnel.
