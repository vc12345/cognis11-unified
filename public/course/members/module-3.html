Module 3: Shared Ratios (The Gap)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+, the examiner often hides the "Total" and only tells you the Gap (e.g., "Person A has £10 more than B"). The secret weapon is realizing that this money only lives in the Extra Boxes. If your child can see that the "Extra £10" belongs to the "2 Extra Boxes," they can find the value of one box and solve the whole puzzle instantly.

The Pocket Money "Money shared 3:1. Person A has £10 more than B. How much does B have?"
The Classroom "The ratio of boys to girls is 5:2. There are 15 more boys than girls. Find the girls."
The Baker's Mix "Flour and sugar are in ratio 7:3. There is 200g more flour than sugar. Find the total mass."

Phase 3: The Engine

Use the sliders with your child to shift the ratio. Watch the Red Bracket—it only cares about the difference between the two people. Help them notice that the value of one box is found by dividing the "Difference Amount" by the "Difference in Boxes."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Total Blindness" Trap: Most children see a number and immediately add the ratio parts together (e.g., 5 + 3 = 8) to divide. This is a disaster if the number is actually a Gap. It makes every box way too small.

The Fix: The "Minus First" Rule. Remind your child: "If you see the words 'more than' or 'difference,' you must subtract the ratio numbers first." (5 - 3 = 2). The "Gap Money" only fits into the "Gap Boxes."
