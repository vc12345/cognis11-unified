Phase 1: Spotting the Pattern

Think of this movement rule simply as Teamwork Speed. When two moving objects travel directly toward one another, they work together as partners to delete the open distance between them. To solve these easily, never look at them as two completely separate journeys. Combine their individual speeds together right away to find out the shared Closing Rate. If Character A travels at 10mph and Character B moves at 15mph, the dividing gap shrinks at a steady rate of 25mph.

Sweets Sharing: "Two cyclists are starting 10 miles apart and cycle directly toward one another, each moving at a steady 5mph. How long before they meet?"
Reading Goals: "A runner speeds along at 10km/h. A walker starts 50km away and travels toward them at 15km/h. Calculate the exact hour they cross paths."
Holiday Budgeting: "Two trains start on opposite sides of a 300-mile track. Train A drives at 60mph, and Train B commutes at 90mph. How long until they pass each other?"

Phase 4: Common Mistake to Watch For

The "Meet Halfway" Trap: Children often mistakenly assume that moving characters will always meet at exactly the 50/50 mark on the track. They overlook the fact that if one train is moving faster, it will consume a much larger chunk of the route.

The Easy Fix: "Speed is Greedy." Remind your child: the faster object always claims more track space. The physical meeting point will invariably sit closer to the slower character's starting position. For instance, if Character A is traveling twice as fast as Character B, it will chew through exactly 2/3 of the road layout on its own.
