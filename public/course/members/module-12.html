Module 12: Relative Speed

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of relative speed as "Teamwork Speed." When two objects move toward each other, they are working together to delete the gap between them. To solve these in the 11+, don't look at them as two separate journeys. Instead, add their speeds together to find the Closing Rate. If Object A moves at 10mph and Object B moves at 15mph, the gap is actually shrinking at 25mph.

The Cyclists "Two cyclists are 10 miles apart. Both travel at 5mph toward each other. When do they meet?"
The Meeting Point "A runner leaves Point A at 10km/h. A walker leaves Point B (50km away) at 15km/h. When do they pass?"
The Rail Impact "Two trains 300 miles apart head toward each other. Train A is 60mph; Train B is 90mph. How long until they pass?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Halfway" Trap: Bright children often assume objects always meet exactly in the middle (50/50). They forget that if one train is faster, it will have covered more miles by the time they meet.

The Fix: "Speed is Greedy." Tell your child: "The faster object eats more of the track." The meeting point will always be closer to the slower object’s starting line. If Train A is twice as fast as Train B, it will gobble up 2/3 of the track, leaving only 1/3 for Train B.
