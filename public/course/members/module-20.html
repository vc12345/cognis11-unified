Module 20: Substitution

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of substitution as a Recipe Swap. You are given a formula (the recipe), but you need to swap the ingredients (the letters) for real numbers. In the 11+, children often get confused when letters and numbers are "glued" together. The secret is knowing that in Algebra, touching means multiplying. If your child can visualize the letter as a container waiting for a value to be poured in, they’ll master this instantly.

The Simple Sum "If $a = 10$ and $b = 3$, what is the value of $a + b$?"
The "Glue" Challenge "If $x = 5$ and $y = 4$, calculate $3x - 2y$."
The 11+ Powerplay "If $p = 4$ and $q = 9$, find the value of $(p^2 + q) \div 5$."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Digit Gluing" Trap: If $A = 5$, many students see "$3A$" and think the answer is $35$. They are treating the letter like a placeholder in a two-digit number!

The Fix: The "Invisible Times" Rule. Remind your child: "Algebra is crowded." If a number and a letter are touching, they are secretly multiplying to save space. You aren't putting the number $5$ next to the $3$; you are multiplying them. Result: $3 \times 5 = 15$. If they touch, they multiply.
