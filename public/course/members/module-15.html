Module 15: Time Conversions

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+, time is the only place where the decimal system fails. We don't live in Base-10 here; we live in Base-60. The examiner is banking on your child assuming that 100 minutes equals an hour. The secret is managing the "Overflow Point." Once a group of minutes hits 60, it ceases to be minutes and must be packaged into a single hour unit.

The Baker "A cake stays in the oven for 85 minutes. How many hours and minutes is that?"
The Traveler "A flight takes 1 hour and 45 minutes. How many total minutes is the journey?"
The Marathon "A runner finishes in 215 minutes. Express this time in Hours and Minutes."

Phase 3: The Engine

Input any number of minutes to see the "Base-60" logic at work. Notice how we use division to find the whole hours and the remainder to find the leftover minutes.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Decimal Mirage" Trap: Most children see 1.5 hours and think it means 1 hour and 50 minutes. They are trying to use the metric system on a clock!

The Fix: The "Half-Tank" Rule. Remind your child: "Time doesn't speak metric." Since the container is 60 minutes wide, 0.5 (half) of the tank is 30 minutes. Always check if their answer is based on 100 or 60. If they use 100, the Saboteur wins.
