Module 24: The Mean Bridge

Foundation Intro

The Tower Leveler

Imagine we have three towers. We want them all to be the exact same height. If we know two towers and we know our goal height, the "Mean Bridge" is just the amount of blocks we need to add to the third tower to make everything level.

"Our goal is to make all three towers 10 blocks high. Tower 1 is 8, Tower 2 is 12. Click 'Level Up' to see how many gold blocks we need to drop into the third spot to make every single tower perfectly equal."

The Goal Pile

When you want to change an average, you must change the Total Pile. If 4 people want to average 5 points each, you need 20 points in the pile. To find what the 4th person must score, just look at the gap between the points you have and the points you need.

"We have 12 coins from 3 friends. We want 4 friends to average 5 coins each. That means our 'Goal Pile' is 20 coins. Look at the ghost coins—that’s the gap! The 4th friend must bring 8 coins to fill the pile."

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of the Mean as the "Fair Share" height. In the 11+, you are often asked: "What must the next person score to change the average?" The secret is looking at the Total Pile. To find the missing person, compare the pile you have now against the pile you need to hit the target. The "Bridge" is the gap between the two totals.

The Mystery "The mean of 3 numbers is 5. Two are 4 and 6. What is the 3rd?"
The Test "Scores: 12, 15, 10. What must the 4th score be to have a mean of 13?"
The Weight "5 kids weigh 40kg on average. A 6th joins and mean becomes 41kg. Find the 6th child's weight."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is the Equality Bridge. On the right is the "Ghost Set"—the target if everyone were equal. On the left is your real group. When you click "Add Missing Data," the missing score drops in to level the sides. The value of that gold bar is your answer.

Phase 3: The Engine

Build a scenario. Notice Step 3: we multiply the New Count by the New Mean to find the big "Target Total." This is the master key.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Average of Averages" Trap: Most children only look at the averages. If the mean is 10 and they want 13, they think they just need a 13. Stop! The 4th person must lift the entire group's average.

The Fix: The "Total Volume" Rule. "Mean is a mask; Total is the truth." Find the New Total ($Count \times Mean$) and subtract the Old Total. The difference is the missing person. **Always find the big totals first.**
