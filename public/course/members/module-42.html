Module 42: Rotational Invariance

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "Mental Acrobatics." In Non-Verbal Reasoning (NVR), a shape is technically identical no matter which way it’s facing—as long as you’ve just turned it. The secret weapon is picking a tiny, unique feature (a Tracker) and checking its position. If your Tracker moves from the "right side" to the "left side" of the main shape, it hasn't been rotated—it’s been mirrored. Learning to spot this difference is how your child will dodge the nastiest traps in the exam.

The Clock Turn "Identify the triangle after a 90° clockwise turn. Does it look the same?"
The Flip vs. Spin "Distinguish between a turned 'L' shape and its mirrored 'Backwards' twin."
The 11+ Mastery "Find the complex shape after a 135° rotation. Keep your eye on the corner dot!"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Mirror" Trap: This is the #1 NVR point-stealer. The examiner shows a shape that has been reflected (flipped like a mirror) but turned to the correct angle. Your child’s brain sees the correct angle and thinks: "That's it!"

The Insider Fix: The "Clockwise Rule." Tell your child: "Trace the features in a circle." In a rotation, the order of details (Pole → Arm → Dot) will always be clockwise. In a reflection, that order will flip to anti-clockwise. If the order breaks, the shape is a fake.
