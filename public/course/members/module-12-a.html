Module 12: Relative Speed

Foundation Intro

The Handshake

When two people walk toward each other, they are working as a Team. If I take 2 steps and you take 3 steps, the distance between us closes by 5 steps total. We add our speeds together to find the "Closing Rate."

"Look at the grid. I’m on one side, you’re on the other. Every time we click 'Walk', we both take our steps. Even though we are walking separately, our steps work together to delete the gap!"

Foundation Progressing

The Teamwork Speed

In the 11+, we combine the two speeds into one Super Speed. If Car A goes at 40mph and Car B goes at 60mph, the gap between them vanishes at 100mph. We treat the whole journey as one car traveling at the Super Speed.

"Look at the bars. Blue is speed A, Gold is speed B. When we put them end-to-end, we get our Teamwork Speed. This is the only speed we need to use in our Magic Triangle!"
Teamwork Speed: 100 mph

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of relative speed as "Teamwork Speed." When two objects move toward each other, they work together to delete the gap. To solve these, don't look at them as two journeys. Add their speeds together to find the Closing Rate. If A moves at 10mph and B moves at 15mph, the gap shrinks at 25mph.

Level 1 "Two cyclists 10 miles apart travel at 5mph toward each other. When do they meet?"
Level 2 "Runner A is at 10km/h. Walker B is 50km away at 15km/h. Find the meeting time."
Level 3 "Trains 300 miles apart. Train A is 60mph; Train B is 90mph. When do they pass?"

Phase 3: The Engine

Set the distance and speeds. Notice how the **Closing Rate** (A + B) determines the timer. Watch the log to see the gap vanish.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Halfway" Trap: Children often assume they meet at exactly 50/50. They forget that if one train is faster, it will cover more ground.

The Fix: "Speed is Greedy." The faster object eats more track. The meeting point is always closer to the slower object's start. If A is twice as fast as B, it takes 2/3 of the track.
